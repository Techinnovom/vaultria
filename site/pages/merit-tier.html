The Merit Tier System

Merit in Vaultria is not rank. It is record. Three tiers, eight earning categories, and a point system that reflects the full picture of a citizen's contribution — including recovery from failure. Assessed at the Veloran. Revisable every two years. Private, but consequential.

Tier 1
Vel Tarin
"The Learning"
0 – 499 points
Entry level. Most citizens begin here. Standard civic access, standard contribution requirement. The foundation from which all advancement begins.
Tier 2
Vel Kadir
"The Knowing"
500 – 1,499 points
Established citizen. Broader access, higher income ceiling, leadership roles available. Achievable through consistent contribution over time — or exceptional early performance.
Tier 3
Vel Sorex
"The Recorded"
1,500+ points
Distinguished citizen. Full civic access, senior domain leadership, Velisar district eligibility. Requires sustained long-term contribution across multiple categories.

Overview

Tiers, Thresholds & Principles

Vel tarin kadir sorex — "From learning to knowing to being recorded"

The Merit Tier System is Vaultria's framework for recognising civic contribution and allocating commensurate access and opportunity. It is not a class system. A T1 citizen and a T3 citizen are equally citizens — they hold the same fundamental rights, the same dignity, and the same protection under the Criminal Code. What differs is access to certain roles, resources, and privileges that the state considers proportional to demonstrated contribution.

Privacy Principle
A citizen's Merit Tier and point total are never publicly disclosed. They appear on the citizen's own CID record and are accessible to the Civic Board and High Tribunal only when relevant to a proceeding. Asking another citizen their tier without invitation is considered one of the stronger social taboos in Vaultrian life. Sharing your own tier is permitted.

The system operates on a point accumulation model across eight earning categories. Points are permanent unless deducted by a violation or decay through inactivity. The annual assessment cycle does not reset points — it adds to them. A citizen who has worked consistently for twenty years carries twenty years of accumulated record.

What makes the Vaultrian system distinct: recovery is built in. A Class II conviction costs 100 points. Five years of consistent contribution earns that back and more. The system is not designed to trap citizens — it is designed to reflect them accurately at any given moment.

Thresholds
T1 → T2 threshold
500 points. Achievable for a dedicated citizen in 6–10 years from a typical Veloran entry of 150–300 points. Faster for citizens who enter with higher Veloran scores or pursue education aggressively.
T2 → T3 threshold
1,500 points. Requires sustained multi-category contribution over a significant period. The average T2 citizen takes 12–20 additional years to reach T3. Some never do. This is acceptable.
Demotion
If a citizen's points fall below their current tier threshold — through violations, skill decay, or contribution failure — a Tier Review is triggered. Demotion is not automatic; the tribunal reviews circumstances before acting.
Tier disclosure in professional context
Employers in restricted domains may request a citizen's tier level (not their point total) as part of a role application. This is the only permitted professional disclosure and requires the citizen's consent.

Initial Assessment

The Veloran Assessment

Vel narovel tarin — "The first record of knowing"

At the Veloran ceremony on a citizen's 18th birthday, a Merit Assessment is conducted alongside the formal citizenship declaration. This assessment reviews everything the citizen has documented up to that point and assigns their opening point total. Not every citizen enters the system at the same level — and Vaultria considers this correct. A citizen who has spent years building skills, serving their community, and maintaining a clean record before their Veloran should enter the system ahead of one who has not.

01
Tarin Record Review
The citizen's complete Tarin schooling record is assessed — academic performance, attendance, documented activities, and any distinctions or commendations received from teachers or sector officials. 0–100 points.
02
Documented Skills
Any certified skills acquired before the Veloran are counted at their standard values. Tier A skills earned before 18 count in full. A citizen who completed a Tier B skill certification at age 16 receives 30 points at their Veloran, as they would at any other assessment.
03
Pre-Citizenship Service
Documented volunteering, community service, and civic participation before the Veloran is assessed at the standard rates. Hours must be registered in the sector record to be counted — informal service without documentation cannot be assessed.
04
Conduct Record
A clean conduct record before the Veloran contributes up to 75 points, scaled by years of clean record. A citizen who has maintained a spotless record since their first Tarin year receives the maximum. Any Civic Notices issued and unresolved reduce this.
05
Commendations
Any commendations received before the Veloran — from teachers, sector officials, or community leaders — count at their standard point values. These are among the rarest Veloran contributions and have significant impact.
Typical Veloran range
100–350 points. Most citizens enter at T1. This is expected and not a failure. T1 is where Vaultrian life begins for the majority of citizens.
T2 entry at Veloran
Requires 500+ points. Achievable with strong Tarin record, multiple certifications, significant volunteering, and a clean record — but uncommon. Fewer than 12% of citizens enter at T2.
T3 entry at Veloran
Requires 1,500+ points — theoretically possible (maximum achievable is ~800) only in exceptional circumstances. In practice, T3 entry at Veloran has never been recorded.
First-generation citizens
Citizens who immigrate and receive citizenship after age 18 undergo an initial assessment at their citizenship grant date. Their pre-Vaultrian record is assessed by the Civic Board and translated to an equivalent point total under Vaultrian criteria.

Point Deductions

Deductions & Penalties

Vel morven kadir — "The record of what was lost"

Points can be lost through criminal violations, civic contribution failures, and skill inactivity. Deductions are applied automatically where the trigger is unambiguous (conviction, missed contribution period) and by tribunal assessment where circumstances require review. No deduction can take a citizen below zero points.

Recovery Note
No deduction except the Class 0 freeze is permanent. A citizen who loses 100 points to a Class II conviction and subsequently maintains a perfect record, completes education, and earns commendations will recover those points within 3–5 years. The system is designed to reflect the citizen accurately at the present moment — not to permanently record their worst moment.

Assessment Schedule

Review Schedule & Process

Vel narovel tarin — "When the record is read again"
Routine Assessment
Every 2 years, automatically. The citizen receives a notification 30 days in advance. Attendance is not required — the system calculates from records. Citizens may submit supplementary documentation within the 30-day window.
Triggered Assessment
Automatically triggered after any criminal conviction, after receiving a Core Council commendation, or after a major qualification change (new degree, domain licence). The citizen is notified within 7 days of the trigger event.
Citizen-Requested Assessment
A citizen may request a reassessment once per calendar year if they believe their record is materially incorrect or does not reflect significant recent activity. No reason is required. The request cannot be denied.
Tier Review
Triggered when a citizen's points fall below their current tier threshold, or when a Class I conviction occurs. The tribunal reviews circumstances before any demotion is applied. A citizen may present their case at this review.
Discretion at Review
Automatic calculations provide the point total. The Civic Board Assessor reviews context — a citizen who lost points due to a serious illness that prevented civic contribution is treated differently to one who simply did not contribute. Documented exceptional circumstances may result in a partial waiver of deductions. This is rare and must be formally justified in the assessment record.

Tier 1 — 0 to 499 Points

Vel Tarin — The Learning

The entry tier. Where Vaultrian life begins.

Vel Tarin is not a lesser tier — it is the correct starting place for the majority of citizens and a legitimate long-term tier for those whose life circumstances make advancement difficult or who choose not to pursue it. The standard Vaultrian life is a T1 life. All fundamental rights are fully intact at T1.

T1 Vel Tarin — Full Privilege List 0 – 499 points
Zone AccessAll civilian and standard civic zones. Infrastructure zones with registered purpose. No access to Tier I restricted areas or above without specific clearance.
VYR Income CeilingStandard band for the employment domain. Set annually by the Civic Board based on domain and zone. No ceiling on savings — only on annual income from civic employment.
HousingStandard allocation queue within home zone. Priority order: family need, tenure, then T1 general queue.
Domain AccessAll general employment domains. Cannot hold mid-level or senior leadership roles in restricted civic domains (healthcare, education, justice, AI systems). Can work within these domains in non-leadership roles.
SponsorshipT1 citizens cannot sponsor new citizens for Vaultrian citizenship.
Kael Days1 per month. Standard allocation. The minimum civic entitlement to unstructured personal time.
Civic AssemblyObserver status. May attend proceedings and submit written observations. Cannot vote on Assembly matters.
Civic Contribution Req.200 hours per year — standard requirement. No additional obligation above this at T1.

Tier 2 — 500 to 1,499 Points

Vel Kadir — The Knowing

The established tier. Broader access earned through sustained contribution.

Vel Kadir represents a citizen who has built a substantial civic record — through employment, skills, service, and consistent contribution over time. The unlocks at T2 are meaningful and practical: more free time, more earning potential, leadership access, and the ability to bring new citizens into Vaultria. T2 is where most ambitious citizens aim as their first significant milestone.

T2 Vel Kadir — Full Privilege List 500 – 1,499 points
Zone AccessAll civilian and infrastructure zones. Tier I restricted areas with advance notification — no special clearance needed, only registration of purpose before entry.
VYR Income Ceiling+25% above T1 ceiling for the same employment domain. Applicable to all registered employment including self-employment.
HousingPriority queue ahead of all T1 applicants in the same zone. In practice this typically reduces wait time for housing allocation by 40–60%.
Domain AccessMid-level leadership roles in Healthcare (Siven), Education (Tarin), Transport (FlowNet). Eligible to apply for Civic Board positions. Eligible for sector mediator certification.
SponsorshipMay sponsor 1 new citizen every 3 years. The sponsor takes civic responsibility for the sponsored citizen's integration period (first 2 years of citizenship). This is a significant commitment and is not taken lightly.
Kael Days2 per month — one additional Kael Day above the T1 standard. In practice this represents 12 extra full personal days per year.
Civic AssemblyFull voting member. May speak, vote, and submit formal motions to the Civic Assembly proceedings.
Civic Contribution Req.220 hours per year — 20 hours above T1 minimum. This reflects the expectation that T2 citizens give somewhat more to the system that gives them somewhat more.

Tier 3 — 1,500+ Points

Vel Sorex — The Recorded

The distinguished tier. A life built entirely in contribution to Vaultria.

Vel Sorex cannot be rushed. It represents a citizen whose entire life record — across skills, service, employment, education, and conduct — is exceptional by any measure. Most citizens who reach T3 do so in their late thirties at earliest and more commonly in their forties or fifties. It is not a young person's tier in most cases. What it unlocks reflects that: the most senior roles, the most significant access, the most personal freedom.

T3 Vel Sorex — Full Privilege List 1,500+ points
Zone AccessAll zones. Tier II restricted areas accessible via clearance request (typically approved within 48 hours for documented purpose). Automatic Morvex border access for approved civic purposes — no advance notification required.
VYR Income Ceiling+50% above T1 ceiling for the same domain. Eligible for the highest income band in any civic employment domain. Senior civic leadership positions are available only to T3 citizens.
HousingFirst-priority queue in any zone. Eligible to apply for Velisar district residential units in Velora — the most sought-after housing in Vaultria. Applications accepted; allocation is not guaranteed but priority is absolute.
Domain AccessSenior leadership in all civic domains. Eligible for Core Council advisory positions. Eligible to head a Velumex Research institution. Eligible for Chief Tribunal Registrar appointment. The highest civic roles in Vaultria are T3-gated.
SponsorshipMay sponsor 1 new citizen per year. The most significant sponsorship capacity in the system. T3 sponsors are trusted with the integration of citizens at a rate that reflects their demonstrated civic commitment.
Kael Days3 per month — two additional Kael Days above the T1 standard. 24 extra personal days per year. The most personal freedom any Vaultrian tier provides.
Civic AssemblySenior member. Eligible to chair Assembly proceedings. Eligible to propose constitutional review motions. The highest civic participation available outside the Vaultcil itself.
Civic Contribution Req.250 hours per year — 50 hours above T1 minimum. The highest contribution requirement — balanced by the highest privileges. T3 citizens give the most. They receive the most.
