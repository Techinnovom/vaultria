Currency system

Main national unit

VYR ⩔

Structured like a real national system: coins (Bits + unit coin) for daily friction, banknotes for trade and salaries, optional high denominations mostly digital. Full denomination list and visual banknote & coin gallery.

Structure overview

  • Coins — low values, transport, small purchases
  • Notes — mid–high value, major transactions

Subunit: Bits

1 VYR = 100 Bits. Bits are the smallest accounting unit; physical coinage maps to Bit fractions up to one full VYR coin.

Naming culture

People do not always say the number:

  • “Give me 2 Vyr.”
  • “That costs a Stack” (100 ⩔).
  • “That’s almost a Core” (1000 ⩔).

Flow

  • Coins → everyday
  • Low notes → daily life
  • Mid notes → business
  • High notes → wealth storage
