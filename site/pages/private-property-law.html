Introduction
Private Property Law of Vaultria
Scope of this law
This law governs all privately owned property within Vaultrian jurisdiction - residential housing, commercial premises, businesses, workshops, warehouses, and any structure not owned or operated by the state.
Scope of this Law
Government-owned buildings are governed separately under the Civic Infrastructure Code. This law applies to all citizens regardless of tier, and to all access holders operating within Vaultrian territory.
Chapter I
Zelek Vel — Kirat lo Birek
Property Ownership — Rights and Obligations
Who may own property in Vaultria, what ownership means, what it does not mean, and what obligations ownership creates. Property in Vaultria is a right. It is not an absolute right. It exists within the frame of the Charter and this law.
Art. X.1
Right to Private Property
Vel nara vel lo Vaultria lo kirat lo zelek vel - house vel, business vel, workshop vel, da vel structure private. Zelek vel lo birek lo Charter vel da Codex vel.
Every citizen of Vaultria has the right to own private property - a house, a business, a workshop, or any private structure. This right is guaranteed by the Charter and governed by this Codex. Ownership is real. It is recorded in the national Property Registry. It is protected by the state. No citizen may be deprived of their property without lawful process, fair compensation, and Tribunal review.
Art. X.2
Who May Own Property
The following persons may own private property in Vaultria:
T1, T2, T3 Citizens - all citizen tiers may own property. The type and location of property available is governed by tier and zone clearance as set out in Chapter VI.
Registered businesses - a legally registered Vaultrian business entity may own the premises from which it operates, subject to commercial property rules in Chapter IV.
Joint ownership - two or more citizens may jointly own property. Joint ownership requires all parties to be registered on the Property Registry. Disputes between joint owners are resolved under Chapter VII.
Access holders (non-citizens) - VEC-C and VEC-P access holders may rent but may not own property. Ownership is a citizen right. Access is permission. They are distinct.
Restriction
VEC-T and VEC-R access holders may not own or rent long-term property. They are accommodated under visitor provisions only.
Art. X.3
The Property Registry
All private property in Vaultria must be registered with the Property Registry, maintained by the Custodian of Citizens. Registration records: the owner's CID, the property address (Node, Sector, Channel), the property type, the purchase price in VYR, the date of transfer, any encumbrances or charges, and the zone classification. An unregistered property is not legally recognised. Transactions on unregistered property are void. Registration is mandatory within 14 days of any ownership transfer.
Offense
Failure to register within 14 days - Class III offense. Deliberate concealment of ownership - Class I offense.
Art. X.4
Ownership Does Not Mean Sovereignty
Owning private property in Vaultria does not grant the owner sovereignty over that property or exemption from Vaultrian law within it. All Vaultrian law applies inside all private property. An owner may set internal rules for their property - conduct codes, entry conditions, operational hours - provided those rules do not violate the Charter, the Codex of Order, or this law. No private space is exempt from lawful enforcement access where a Class I or Class 0 offense is suspected and a Resolution Chamber warrant has been issued.
Art. X.5
The Anti-Rentierism Principle Applied to Private Property
Da vel nara lo derive lo passive income lo Vaultria da-active contribution. Zelek vel lo earned - da extracted.
The Charter's prohibition of passive extraction applies to private property. No citizen may own property solely for the purpose of extracting value from it without active contribution to Vaultrian society. Specifically: a citizen who owns more than three residential properties must demonstrate active civic contribution at T2 level or above, or justify the holdings as a registered commercial enterprise. Properties held in excess of this threshold without justification are subject to a Progressive Property Levy assessed by the Custodian of Economy.
Clarification
Owning and renting property is not prohibited. It is legitimate commerce. What is prohibited is accumulating property as a passive extraction mechanism while contributing nothing to Vaultria. A citizen who owns ten properties and employs staff, maintains them, and pays contribution tax is fully compliant. A citizen who owns ten properties, contributes nothing, and extracts rent is in violation of this article.
Art. X.6
State Acquisition - Compulsory Purchase
The state may acquire private property for legitimate civic purposes - infrastructure, national security, public space - through compulsory purchase. The following conditions must all be met:
The acquisition must be authorised by the Core Council by majority vote
The purpose must be documented and publicly stated
Fair compensation must be offered at current market value in VYR before the acquisition takes effect
The affected owner has the right to appeal to the High Tribunal within 30 days
No acquisition may take place during the appeal period unless a Class 0 emergency is active
Chapter II
Lunok Vel — Kirat lo Kala
Housing — The Right to Shelter
The Homelessness Principle
New citizens enter Vaultria without housing. This is not a punishment - it is the starting condition. The state does not provide housing automatically. Citizens must earn VYR and acquire housing through the legitimate property market. Civic housing exists as a safety net only, not a default. The expectation is that a T1 citizen in employment will be able to rent within their first 30-60 days of active citizenship.
Art. XI.1
Residential Property Types
Residential property in Vaultria falls into four categories:
Standard residential - apartments, houses, and dwelling units available for rent or purchase by all citizen tiers in Civic Grid zones. The most common housing type.
Premium residential - larger dwellings, higher specification, located in desirable sectors. Available to T2 and T3 citizens. Price reflects quality and location.
Civic housing - state-owned dwellings allocated on a waiting list to citizens who cannot yet afford market rent. Not a right - a provision. Citizens on civic housing are expected to move to private rental within 180 days of stable employment. See Article XI.6.
Restricted residential - housing in restricted zones (Kaedron, Nerion, Draven, Morvex). Allocated by role, not by choice. Citizens stationed in these zones are assigned housing by their division. Not available on the open market.
Art. XI.2
Purchasing Residential Property
A citizen wishing to purchase residential property must:
Hold active citizenship at T1 or above
Have sufficient Core VYR balance to cover the full purchase price or to satisfy the terms of a Property Credit Agreement (see Article XI.3)
Pass a zone clearance check - the property must be in a zone the citizen is cleared to occupy at their current tier
Register the purchase with the Property Registry within 14 days
Notify Vaultryn Core of the new address - this updates the citizen's access identity and movement profile
Art. XI.3
Property Credit Agreement
A citizen who cannot pay the full purchase price outright may enter a Property Credit Agreement with the Aegis Treasury. Terms:
Minimum deposit: 15% of purchase price in Core VYR
Agreement term: 10, 20, or 30 years
Interest rate: set by the Custodian of Economy, published monthly, applied uniformly - no citizen receives a preferential rate on personal grounds
Missed payments: three consecutive missed payments trigger a review. Five consecutive missed payments trigger a Tribunal-authorised repossession process with 90 days notice and rehousing assistance
A citizen may pay off their Agreement early with no penalty
Note
Property Credit Agreements are with the Aegis Treasury only. Private lending for property purchase by individuals or unregistered entities is a Class II offense.
Art. XI.4
Selling Residential Property
A citizen wishing to sell their residential property must:
Declare the sale to the Property Registry before it is completed
Settle any outstanding Property Credit Agreement balance from the proceeds before distribution of remaining funds
Pay a Property Transfer Contribution of 4% of the sale price to the national treasury - the equivalent of a stamp duty, funding civic infrastructure
Ensure the buyer meets the zone clearance requirements for the property
Transfer the Registry title within 14 days of sale completion
Art. XI.5
Inheritance of Property
When a citizen dies, their registered property passes under the following rules:
If a valid Will is registered with the Property Registry, the property passes to the named beneficiary, provided the beneficiary is a Vaultrian citizen and meets the zone clearance requirements
If no Will exists, property passes to the citizen's registered next of kin as recorded in the CID system
If no qualifying heir exists, the property transfers to the state at assessed market value with that value credited to the citizen's estate
Non-citizen heirs may not inherit Vaultrian property. The property is sold at market value and the proceeds are held for 24 months while the heir may pursue citizenship. If citizenship is not obtained within 24 months the proceeds transfer to the national treasury
Art. XI.6
Civic Housing - Conditions and Limits
Civic housing is a state provision, not an entitlement. Citizens are placed on the Civic Housing Waitlist upon registration if they declare homelessness. The following conditions apply:
Placement is by waitlist position, not by tier. T1 citizens are not disadvantaged relative to T2 in accessing civic housing
Civic housing is allocated in the citizen's declared preferred zone, subject to availability
Civic housing rent is set at 20% of the citizen's current monthly VYR income - adjusted automatically if income changes
Citizens in civic housing who achieve stable employment above the civic housing income threshold are expected to transition to private rental within 180 days. The state provides a transition allowance of ⩔500 to assist with this move
A citizen may not own other property while occupying civic housing
Civic housing may not be sublet. Subletting civic housing is a Class II offense and results in immediate termination of the civic housing allocation
Chapter III
Shen Vel — Kirat lo Nara lo Lunok
Tenancy Law - The Rights of Renters and Landlords
The complete law governing the rental of private property. Both tenant and landlord have rights. Both have obligations. Neither may use the arrangement to exploit the other.
Art. XII.1
The Tenancy Agreement
Every rental arrangement for residential or commercial property must be governed by a registered Tenancy Agreement. The Agreement must be:
In writing and in Vaelic - the official language of all legal documents in Vaultria. An English translation may be provided alongside for the tenant's reference but the Vaelic text governs
Registered with the Property Registry within 7 days of commencement
Signed by both parties with their CID number recorded
Specifying: the property address, the agreed rent in VYR per month, the term of the agreement, the notice period for termination, the deposit amount, and any specific conditions agreed between the parties
Offense
Unregistered tenancy - Class III. Operating a rental without a Tenancy Agreement - Class II. Providing a false agreement - Class I.
Art. XII.2
Tenant Rights - What a Tenant Is Guaranteed
Every tenant in Vaultria is guaranteed the following rights regardless of what the Tenancy Agreement says. These rights cannot be contracted away:
Right to habitable condition - the property must be safe, structurally sound, weatherproof, and in working order at the start of the tenancy. Any defect the landlord knew of and did not disclose entitles the tenant to remedy or to terminate the agreement with no penalty
Right to quiet enjoyment - the landlord may not enter the property without the tenant's permission except in an emergency. Non-emergency entry requires 48 hours notice in writing
Right against unlawful eviction - a tenant may not be evicted without a formal termination notice, the legally required notice period, and where contested, a Resolution Chamber ruling. Changing locks, removing possessions, or cutting utilities to force departure is unlawful eviction - a Class I offense
Right to deposit return - the deposit must be returned within 14 days of the tenancy end unless the landlord has documented evidence of damage beyond normal wear and uses the dispute process in Chapter VII
Right to rent stability - rent may not be increased more than once per 12-month period and may not be increased by more than the annual Vaultrian Property Index rate published by the Custodian of Economy
Right to maintenance - structural repairs, plumbing, heating, and essential systems are the landlord's responsibility. The tenant is responsible for minor maintenance and for damage they cause
Art. XII.3
Landlord Rights - What a Landlord Is Guaranteed
Every landlord in Vaultria is guaranteed the following rights:
Right to timely rent - rent is due on the agreed date. Late payment of more than 7 days constitutes a breach. Persistent late payment (three times in a 12-month period) is grounds for termination with the required notice period
Right to property condition - the tenant must return the property in the condition they received it, accounting for normal wear. Damage beyond normal wear may be claimed from the deposit or pursued through the Resolution Chamber
Right to termination - a landlord may terminate a tenancy with proper notice. Standard notice periods: short-term agreement (under 12 months) - 30 days notice; annual agreement - 60 days notice; long-term agreement (over 3 years) - 90 days notice. Notice must be in writing and registered with the Property Registry
Right to select tenants - a landlord may choose their tenants subject to one restriction: a landlord may not refuse a tenant on the basis of their zone of origin, prior homelessness status, or T1 tier alone. Refusal must be based on legitimate grounds - financial capacity, references, property suitability
Chapter IV
Birek Vel — Kirat lo Kavel
Commercial Property and Business Law
The rules governing businesses, commercial premises, trading, and private enterprise in Vaultria. Commerce is encouraged. Exploitation is not. Every business that operates in Vaultria contributes to it.
Art. XIII.1
Registering a Business
Any citizen wishing to operate a business in Vaultria must register that business with the Custodian of Economy. Registration requires:
The business name - must be in Vaelic or contain at least a Vaelic element. Purely foreign-language business names are not permitted on civic signage
The type of business - categorised by domain (commerce, food, technology, education, health, services, manufacturing, research, media, security)
The registered owner's CID number
The registered premises address or, for mobile or digital businesses, the declared operating node
Confirmation of the VYR business wallet - separate from the owner's personal wallet
A declaration that the business complies with this law and the Codex of Order
Offense
Operating a business without registration - Class II. Providing false registration information - Class I.
Art. XIII.2
Commercial Premises
A registered business may own or rent the premises from which it operates subject to the following:
Commercial premises must be in a zone where the business type is permitted. Residential zones have restrictions on commercial activity - see the Zone Use Table in Chapter VI
The premises must be registered with the Property Registry as a commercial property. Operating a business from residential property is permitted only for home-based digital or service businesses, and only if the residential designation of the property is not compromised
All external signage must be in Vaelic. A secondary translation may appear below in smaller text
Business premises must conform to the building standards in Chapter V
Art. XIII.3
Contribution Tax
All businesses operating in Vaultria pay a flat Contribution Tax on all revenue. The rate is set annually by the Custodian of Economy and applies uniformly across all business types and sizes. There are no exemptions, no sector carve-outs, no preferential rates for connected parties. The rate is publicly posted. It is the same for a market stall and for the largest enterprise in Vaultria. Contribution Tax is paid monthly through the VYR business wallet. Non-payment for two consecutive months triggers an automatic review and asset freeze.
Offense
Non-payment of Contribution Tax - Class II escalating to Class I for persistent non-payment. Tax evasion by concealment of revenue - Class I.
Art. XIII.4
Employment - Obligations of Business Owners
A business that employs Vaultrian citizens must:
Pay all wages in Core VYR on the agreed payment schedule. Payment in any currency other than VYR is not permitted within Vaultrian jurisdiction
Maintain an employment record for each worker - registered with the Custodian of Citizens. This record is part of the worker's trust score assessment
Provide minimum working conditions as set by the Custodian of Citizens - these are the Vaultrian equivalent of labour standards and are revised annually
Not employ any citizen below T1 active status. Employment of unregistered persons - those without active citizenship or valid access status - is a Class I offense
Provide the worker with a written statement of terms within 7 days of employment commencement
Chapter V
Velaron Birek — Kirat lo Torakav
Building Standards - Private Structures in Vaultrian Territory
Every building in Vaultria - private or civic - is part of Vaultrian territory. Private buildings do not need to look identical to civic buildings. But they must respect the Vaultrian built environment. These standards protect the character of Vaultria and the safety of citizens within it.
Art. XIV.1
Planning Permission
No private building may be constructed, substantially modified, demolished, or changed in use without a Building Permit issued by the Infrastructure Division of the Custodian of Infrastructure. Applications are reviewed within 21 days. A Permit is issued, refused with reasons, or referred for additional assessment. Construction without a Permit is halted immediately by the Infrastructure Division and the unpermitted structure may be required to be removed at the owner's cost.
Offense
Construction without a Permit - Class II. Continuing construction after a stop order - Class I.
Art. XIV.2
The Vaultrian Private Building Code
Private buildings in Vaultria are not required to follow the full Vaultrian Civic Architectural Style (which applies to government and civic structures). However, the following minimum standards apply to all private buildings:
Material quality - all external materials must be durable, weather-appropriate, and must not degrade in a way that creates hazard or visual blight within 10 years. Cheap materials that deteriorate rapidly are not permitted on any external surface visible from a public channel
Signage in Vaelic - any external signage, business name, or street-facing text must appear in Vaelic as the primary script. This applies to all private commercial properties. Residential properties are not required to display signage
Height limits - private buildings may not exceed the height guidelines set for their zone. No private structure may be taller than the tallest civic building in the same node without a special height exemption from the Custodian of Infrastructure
Structural safety - all buildings must meet the structural safety standards published by the Infrastructure Division. Periodic safety inspections are required for commercial premises every 5 years and residential properties every 10 years
No V-arch ornamental use - the V-arch form is the structural language of Vaultrian civic architecture. Private buildings may incorporate V-forms structurally, but may not apply the V-arch decoratively in a manner that implies civic authority. Purely decorative V-arch facades on private buildings require Infrastructure Division approval
Art. XIV.3
Renovation and Modification
Internal renovations that do not affect external appearance, structural integrity, or property classification do not require a Building Permit - only a Renovation Notification filed with the Property Registry within 30 days of commencement. External changes - new windows, changed facade materials, extensions, additions - always require a full Building Permit. Historic structures designated by the Language Authority or the Infrastructure Division as being of Vaultrian cultural significance may not be externally modified without special heritage approval.
Art. XIV.4
Safety Obligations of Property Owners
Every property owner in Vaultria is responsible for the safety of their building. This includes:
Maintaining the structure in a condition that does not endanger occupants, neighbours, or persons on public channels adjacent to the property
Responding to Infrastructure Division safety notices within the timeframe specified - urgent safety notices within 48 hours, standard notices within 30 days
Allowing safety inspection access to the Infrastructure Division and to medical and emergency services at all times
Where a property is found to be an immediate safety hazard, the Infrastructure Division may close it and require the owner to vacate and repair. Costs of emergency remediation where the owner fails to act are charged to the owner as a debt to the state
Chapter VI
Zelek Birek — Kirat lo Doro
Zone Classification - Where Private Property May Exist
Not all zones permit all types of private property. This chapter defines where citizens may live, where businesses may operate, and what is restricted by zone.
Zone Classification Principle
Private property exists in the zones where it is permitted. The zone classification is not a suggestion - it is the law. A business operating in a zone that does not permit its type will be closed. A citizen living in a zone above their clearance level will be required to vacate. These are not punishments. They are the conditions of the zone.

Art. XV.1
Zone Change Applications
A citizen who wishes to live or operate a business in a zone above their current clearance level may apply for a Zone Access Upgrade. The application is submitted to the Custodian of Citizens and reviewed against the citizen's trust score, tier, contribution record, and the specific requirements of the target zone. Approval is not guaranteed. Refusal is documented and the citizen may reapply after 90 days. A citizen granted Zone Access Upgrade maintains that access while their tier and conduct justify it - it may be reviewed and withdrawn.
Art. XV.2
Restricted Zone Violations
A citizen found to be living or operating in a zone they are not cleared for will receive a formal notice from the Custodian of Citizens requiring them to vacate or cease operations within 30 days. If they have a legitimate tenancy they are compensated for the unexpired term of their agreement by the state. If the violation was deliberate - the citizen knew they were not cleared for the zone - it constitutes a zonal access offense under Codex Title III.
Offense
Accidental zone violation - notice and required relocation, no further penalty. Deliberate zone violation - Class II. Deliberate violation of a Red or Black zone - Class I or Class 0.
Chapter VII
Kirel Vel — Da-Kirat lo Zelek
Property Disputes - Resolution
When parties disagree about property - ownership, condition, rent, boundaries, damages - this is the process. Disputes are resolved. They do not fester. Vaultrian property law has no jurisdiction vacuum.
Art. XVI.1
The Property Resolution Process
All private property disputes follow this process in order:
Stage 1 - Direct negotiation (0-14 days): The parties attempt to resolve the dispute directly.
Stage 2 - Property Mediation (14-45 days): A state-appointed mediator assists where direct negotiation fails.
Stage 3 - Resolution Chamber (45+ days): Binding decision after evidence review.
Stage 4 - High Tribunal appeal: For legal-principle disputes only.
Art. XVI.2

Art. XVI.3
Costs of Dispute
Property mediation is free to both parties. Resolution Chamber proceedings have a filing fee of ⩔50 from each party. High Tribunal appeals have a filing fee of ⩔200.
Chapter VIII
Drak Vel — Kirat lo Kirat
Enforcement - How This Law Is Upheld
This law has teeth. The institutions that enforce it, the penalties that apply, and the rights citizens have when enforcement is brought against them.
Art. XVII.1
Enforcement Bodies
This law is enforced by Property Registry, Infrastructure Division, and Commerce Division in their respective domains.
Art. XVII.2
Vaultryn Core Monitoring
Vaultryn Core flags anomalies for human review; it does not impose automatic sanctions.
Art. XVII.3
Rights During Enforcement
Right to be informed of allegations and evidence.
Right to respond before further action except emergency safety cases.
Right to appeal through Resolution Chamber process.
Right to correction and compensation for enforcement error.

Art. XVII.5
This Law and the Charter
Tovatek Kirat lo da navol lo lor. Lo law vel - lo vel Private Property Law - lo Charter vel.
This law operates beneath the Charter. Where any provision of this law conflicts with the National Charter, the Charter governs. The right to private property and right against unlawful deprivation remain Charter-protected.
