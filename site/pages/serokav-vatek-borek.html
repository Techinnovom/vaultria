◈ Vatek Börek

VATEK-SEROKAV · PASTRY OF THE MACHINE

Spiced Street

Layers of thin yufka pastry filled with spiced ground lamb, pine nuts, and a touch of dried currant — rolled or folded into long cigars or squares and baked until the outside is golden and shatters when you bite. Sold from warm trays in every district from morning through evening. The cold yoghurt beside it is non-negotiable — the contrast between the flaky hot pastry and the cold, slightly sour dip is the whole experience. Nothing about the Vatek Börek is accidental.

⏱ 50 min · 👥 Makes 12 · 🌡 200 °C oven

Ingredients

  • 12 sheets yufka or filo pastry · 80ml melted butter
  • 400g ground lamb · 1 onion, finely diced · 2 garlic cloves
  • 50g pine nuts, toasted · 30g dried currants
  • 1 tsp cumin · 1 tsp allspice · ½ tsp cinnamon · salt
  • Fresh flat-leaf parsley — generous handful, chopped
  • 1 egg yolk + milk for wash · sesame seeds
  • Cold yoghurt with dried mint to serve

Method

  1. Cook onion and garlic in a dry pan until soft. Add lamb, break it up well, cook until no pink remains.
  2. Add all spices. Stir through pine nuts and currants. Remove from heat, add parsley. Cool completely.
  3. Brush one sheet of pastry lightly with butter. Place a second sheet on top. Brush again.
  4. Spoon filling along one edge in a thin line. Roll up tightly into a cigar. Press ends shut.
  5. Arrange on a buttered tray. Brush tops with egg wash. Scatter sesame seeds.
  6. Bake at 200 °C for 22–25 minutes until deeply golden. Serve warm with cold yoghurt.
