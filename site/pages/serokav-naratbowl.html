◈ Naratbowl

NARAT-SEROKAV · BODY FOOD

Mild Optional heat

The foundational meal of Vaultrian daily life. Slow-cooked grain — similar to fonio or millet — topped with a rich vegetable stew of tomatoes, peppers, sweet potato and leafy greens. Named for the Vaelic word for body, because this is what keeps you strong. Every household has their own version. The stew recipe is passed down, adjusted, argued over.

⏱ 45 min · 👥 Serves 4 · 🌡 Medium

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fonio or millet, rinsed
  • 3 large tomatoes, blended
  • 2 red bell peppers + 1 scotch bonnet
  • 1 large sweet potato, cubed
  • Handful of spinach or bitter leaf
  • 1 onion, sliced · palm oil or groundnut oil
  • 1 tbsp locust beans (iru)
  • Spice blend: crayfish, ginger, clove, black pepper · salt

Method

  1. Cook fonio in salted water 15 minutes until fluffy. Cover and set aside.
  2. Heat oil. Fry onion until golden, add locust beans, fry 2 minutes.
  3. Pour in blended tomato and peppers. Fry down 12 minutes until oil rises.
  4. Add sweet potato, spice blend and 1 cup water. Cover, simmer 15 minutes.
  5. Fold in greens, cook 3 more minutes. Adjust salt.
  6. Serve stew over grain in a deep bowl — stew sits on top, not mixed in.
