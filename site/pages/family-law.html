Codex Vol. V · Preamble

Preamble & Governing Principles

Vel sena kadir — "The law of the joined"

The Vaultrian Family Law Codex governs the legal framework of union, parenthood, and family dissolution. It proceeds from the recognition that families are the primary unit of Vaultrian life — not the state, not the zone, not the sector. The state's role in family life is precisely defined and deliberately limited: to record, to protect, and to resolve what families cannot resolve themselves.

This Codex operates alongside but independently of the Traditions document, which describes the cultural and ceremonial practices of Vaultrian family life. The law governs rights and obligations. The traditions describe how citizens choose to live within those rights. Neither overrides the other.

Absolute Equality of Union
No article of this Codex distinguishes between unions based on the gender, sexuality, background, zone, or merit tier of the parties. The word "union" in this Codex means any registered Sena Narovel between two eligible citizens. All references to rights, obligations, and procedures apply equally to all unions without exception.

Article I

Union Registration & Rights

Sena Narovel — "The recorded joining"

A Vaultrian union is a civic registration. Two citizens file a Joint Citizen Declaration at any Civic Board, their CIDs are linked in the national registry, and they become a recognised legal household. The ceremony, the celebration, and everything else are the citizens' own business. The law requires only the registration.

Art. I.4Line Name Declaration

Within 30 days of registration, the couple must file a Line Name Declaration with the Civic Board. Three options are available:

If no declaration is filed within 30 days, Option A is applied automatically. The declaration can be amended once — any subsequent amendment requires a tribunal review.

Article II

Dissolution

Vel sena morven — "The ending of the joined"

Dissolution is a right, not a privilege. No citizen may be prevented from ending a registered union. No reason is required. No fault is assigned. The state does not determine who was right or wrong in the ending of a marriage — it records the ending and resolves what follows.

Art. II.1Standard Dissolution
01
Filing
Either partner files a Dissolution Declaration at any Civic Board. Both do not need to be present. The filing party's CID is sufficient. The other party is notified digitally within 24 hours.
02
30-Day Reflection Period
From the filing date, a 30-day reflection period begins. During this period the union is not yet dissolved. Housing rights remain shared. All financial entitlements remain intact. Neither party may remove the other from the registered household.
03
Finalisation
After 30 days, dissolution is finalised automatically. No appearance required. If both parties agree to withdraw, they file a joint withdrawal before the 30 days expire and the union continues.
04
Asset Resolution
If assets are uncontested, the default division rules apply automatically. If assets are contested, either party may file for tribunal mediation within 60 days of finalisation.
Art. II.2Immediate Dissolution — No Reflection Period

The 30-day reflection period is waived in the following circumstances:

Documented domestic abuse. Filed by the harmed party, or by the Civic Board on their behalf. Immediate dissolution plus Protective Order.Same-day
Abandonment. One party has left the registered household and cannot be located for 90 or more consecutive days.After 90 days
Tribunal emergency order. In extreme circumstances at tribunal discretion, immediate dissolution may be ordered.By order
Art. II.3Asset Division on Dissolution

Article III

Children's Legal Status & Parentage

Vel narovel kadir — "The first record of the new citizen"

A child is a Vaultrian citizen from the moment of their Narovel registration, not from birth, not from the Veloran, and not from any parental decision. The Charter's protections apply immediately. Parents hold guardianship — a civic responsibility with defined obligations — but the child's citizenship is their own and cannot be revoked by their parents.

Art. III.1Citizenship at Birth
Born in Vaultria, at least one Vaultrian parentAutomatic citizen — registration within 7 days required
Born outside Vaultria, at least one Vaultrian parentMay register within 1 year of birth — same rights on registration
Born in Vaultria to non-citizen parentsEligible for residency — citizenship requires formal application at age 18
CID on registrationIssued immediately — inactive status until Veloran
Art. III.2Parentage Registration

Parentage is declared at Narovel. Up to two parents may be registered. Being in a registered union is not required — two unregistered co-parents hold identical legal standing to a registered couple for all purposes relating to the child.

Two registered parentsEqual parental rights from registration — neither parent is primary by default
One registered parent (sole)Full parental rights — no distinction in any civic system
Unknown second parentOne parent registers alone — carries no civic disadvantage for the child
Disputed parentageResolved by zone tribunal — biological evidence may be presented but is not the sole determining factor
Children Are Not Property
Parents hold guardianship — not ownership. Guardianship is a civic responsibility that includes the obligation to register the child, ensure their education, and protect their welfare. It does not include the right to determine the child's beliefs, prevent them from accessing civic systems, or override their rights under the Charter.

Article IV

Custody & Guardianship

Vel kadir sena narovel — "The continued responsibility"

Dissolution ends the union between two adults. It does not end either parent's relationship with their children. Both registered parents retain full parental rights and responsibilities after dissolution unless the tribunal has explicitly removed them. The child's welfare is the sole determining factor in all custody decisions — not the preferences of either parent, not the circumstances of the dissolution.

Art. IV.1Default Position — Shared Custody

Where parents dissolve a union and have children, the default legal position is shared custody. The child has two legal homes. Neither parent is presumed primary. Parents may agree on any arrangement and file it with the Civic Board — filed arrangements are legally binding and enforceable.

If parents cannot agree, the zone tribunal mediates using Vel Sena process first. If mediation fails, the tribunal determines the arrangement based solely on the child's welfare.

Art. IV.2Sole Custody — Conditions

Sole custody — removing one parent's access entirely — is granted only in defined circumstances. It is not a default remedy and is not granted based on one parent's preference alone.

One parent is in Restricted Zone ConfinementAutomatic sole custody review
One parent has caused documented harm to the childSole custody granted pending full review
One parent has abandoned the child — 90+ days no contactTribunal may grant sole custody
One parent voluntarily relinquishes parental rightsFormal filing required — irrevocable without tribunal review
Art. IV.3The Child's Voice

Children are citizens. Their expressed preferences in custody proceedings are taken seriously and are weighted by age.

Under 12
Welfare assessed by the tribunal's appointed child welfare officer. The child may be spoken with but does not give formal preference.
Age 12–15
Child may formally express a preference to the tribunal. Preference is considered but is not determinative. Tribunal weighs it against welfare evidence.
Age 16–17
Preference carries significant weight. Tribunal requires strong welfare grounds to override the preference of a 16-year-old.
Age 17
Preference is presumed correct unless specific welfare evidence directly contradicts it. A 17-year-old's expressed preference will almost always be followed.
Art. IV.4Relocation After Dissolution
Relocation within same zonePermitted without consent or approval
Relocation more than one zone awayRequires written consent of other parent or tribunal approval
Relocation outside VaultriaRequires tribunal approval and written consent of other parent — unless sole custody has been granted
Relocation without consent or approvalConstitutes violation of custody arrangement — subject to tribunal order and potential Class II offense

Article V

Inheritance

Vel morven kadir — "What remains after"

Vaultrian inheritance law operates on two tracks: the Vel Morven Directive — the citizen's own declared wishes — overrides everything. Where no Directive exists, the default order applies. There is no inheritance tax. Inheritance is not income. A child under 18 can never be fully disinherited.

Art. V.2With a Vel Morven Directive

A citizen's Vel Morven Directive — their advance declaration of wishes — overrides the default order completely. A citizen may leave their estate to any person or institution in any proportion. There is no restriction on who may inherit.

Overrides default orderCompletely — Directive is the law
May leave estate to non-family membersPermitted without restriction
Exception — minor children. Children under 18 cannot be fully disinherited. They are entitled to a minimum 10% of the estate regardless of what the Directive states.10% minimum
Inheritance taxNone — Vaultria does not tax inheritance

Art. V.3Minor Children's Inheritance

Assets inherited by a child under 18 are held in a Civic Trust managed by the surviving parent, or — if none — by a Civic Guardian appointed by the tribunal. The child has no access to the Trust funds until their Veloran at age 18, at which point full control transfers to them automatically.

The Civic Guardian may use Trust funds for the child's welfare, education, and reasonable needs during the guardianship period. All Trust expenditure is logged and presented to the child at the Veloran.

Article VI

Adoption & Non-Biological Parentage

Vel narovel sena — "The chosen record"

Adoption in Vaultria creates full legal parentage equivalent in every respect to biological parentage. An adopted child is a citizen with the same rights, the same inheritance entitlements, and the same civic standing as any other. The process is thorough but not onerous — Vaultria does not make adoption difficult.

Art. VI.1Adoption — Eligibility & Process

Any Vaultrian citizen or registered couple may adopt. No restriction on gender, merit tier, marital status, or zone. Minimum age: 21. No maximum age.

01
Application
Filed at the Civic Board. Both parties if a couple. Background documentation submitted — no invasive personal investigation is conducted at this stage.
02
Home Assessment
A sector welfare officer visits the household once. Assessment covers suitability and safety — not lifestyle, beliefs, or personal choices that do not affect child welfare.
03
Matching Period
Six-month period during which a suitable child is identified. The prospective parent(s) may express preferences. Matching is done by the Civic Board with the child's welfare as the primary criterion.
04
Supervised Integration
90-day period during which the child lives with the adoptive family under light civic supervision. Either party may withdraw during this period without legal consequence.
05
Full Legal Parentage
Registered at the Civic Board. The adoptive parent(s) hold all parental rights and responsibilities equivalent to birth registration. The child's line name may be updated if the family chooses.
Original Birth Record
The adopted child's original birth record is sealed in the Permanent Archive — not deleted, not destroyed. It is inaccessible to the public. The child may access it themselves at age 18. This right cannot be waived by the adoptive parents.
Art. VI.2Step-Parentage

A registered partner of a child's parent may apply to become a co-registered parent — a step-parent registration. This requires the written consent of the existing registered parent.

Does step-parent registration remove the other biological parent's rights?No — unless that parent voluntarily relinquishes them by formal filing
Maximum registered parentsTwo — step-parent registration replaces an absent parent's slot or is added only if one slot is vacant

Article VII

Domestic Abuse & Protective Orders

Vel morven kadir sena — "Protecting the family from within"

Vaultria recognises that the most dangerous place for a citizen can be their own home. The family-first principle does not mean the state is blind to harm within families. Where harm is present, the state acts immediately and decisively — and the harmed party is never the one who must leave.

Art. VII.1Definition of Domestic Abuse

Domestic abuse in Vaultrian law includes but is not limited to the following. Physical injury is not required for abuse to be legally recognised.

Physical violence of any kindAlways recognised
Sustained psychological abuse — threats, humiliation, fearRecognised
Financial control — restricting or controlling access to VYRRecognised
Isolation from civic life — preventing participation in civic systemsRecognised
Coercive control — a pattern of behaviour designed to dominateRecognised
Art. VII.2The Protective Order — Vel Morven Kadir

A Protective Order is filed at any Civic Board or directly with a tribunal. It may be filed by any of the following:

◉
The harmed party — always available, no evidence threshold required to file
◉
The Civic Board on the harmed party's behalf — when the harmed party cannot or will not file
◉
A Vel Sena mediator who identifies harm indicators during proceedings
◉
A healthcare provider (Vel Siven) who identifies harm in a patient
Order Effects
Mandatory separation. The abusive party leaves the shared residence. The harmed party is never the one who leaves.Immediate
CID proximity restriction. Abusive party cannot come within the tribunal-defined range of the harmed party's registered address.Immediate
Immediate dissolution option. Harmed party may request immediate dissolution — 30-day reflection period is waived.On request
Access to children suspended pending tribunal review of custody situation.Immediate

The Protective Order cannot be withdrawn by pressure from any third party. The harmed citizen alone may withdraw it. The Civic Board may refuse to withdraw an Order if they believe the withdrawal is being made under duress.

Art. VII.3Children in Abusive Households

If documented abuse is occurring in a household where children are present, a child welfare assessment is triggered automatically — regardless of whether the adult harmed party has filed or whether they wish to proceed with action.

Children are never left in a household where documented abuse is occurring. Emergency guardian placement is available within 24 hours when required. Emergency placement does not require tribunal approval — a Civic Board officer may arrange it immediately and file for tribunal review within 72 hours.

Criminal Overlap
Domestic abuse is simultaneously a family law matter and a criminal matter. Physical harm is minimum Class II under Codex Vol. IV — potentially Class I. Coercive control is Class II. The family law Protective Order and the criminal proceeding run in parallel — resolution of one does not close the other. The harmed party cannot be required to choose between them.

Article VIII

Surrogacy & Assisted Reproduction

Vel narovel sena kadir — "The arranged recording of new life"

Vaultria permits both surrogacy and assisted reproduction in all forms. The legal framework is designed to protect three parties simultaneously: the intended parent(s), the surrogate, and the child. Where these interests conflict, the child's welfare takes precedence. Where the surrogate's and intended parents' interests conflict, the law distinguishes between before and after 20 weeks of gestation.

Art. VIII.1Surrogacy — Gestational & Traditional

Both gestational surrogacy (surrogate carries embryo not biologically hers) and traditional surrogacy (surrogate's own egg used) are permitted. Surrogacy agreements must be filed with the Civic Board before the process begins. Verbal agreements have no legal standing in Vaultrian surrogacy law.

Legal parent at birth: the intended parent(s) named in the filed agreement — not the surrogateFrom Narovel registration
Surrogate's parental claim after birthNone — unless agreement is voided (see below)
Surrogate's medical autonomy throughout pregnancyFull and unrestricted
Withdrawal right — before 20 weeks: Surrogate may withdraw from the agreement. Legal parentage reverts to the surrogate until adoption proceedings initiated by intended parent(s).Before 20 weeks only
After 20 weeks: Agreement is binding on both parties. Withdrawal is no longer available.Binding from 20 weeks
Compensation for surrogatePermitted — commercial surrogacy is legal. Maximum: 12 months of the surrogate's income ceiling. Cannot exceed this regardless of agreement terms.
Art. VIII.2Assisted Reproduction — Donation

Sperm, egg, and embryo donation are all permitted. All donations must be registered with the Karelth Medical Authority before use.

Donor parental rights — defaultNone. A registered donor has no parental rights or obligations unless a separate legal agreement explicitly grants them.
Donor financial obligationsNone by default — cannot be assigned retrospectively
Anonymous donation: Not permitted. All donors are registered under a confidential reference number.Prohibited
Donor-conceived child's right to identifying information: At age 18, the child may access identifying donor information. This right cannot be waived by parents or donors.Absolute — at age 18
Child's access to donor information before age 18Non-identifying medical and genetic information only — accessible at any age via the child's guardian
Why Anonymous Donation Is Prohibited
Vaultria's position is that a person has the right to know their full biological origin. This is consistent with the adopted child's right to access their original birth record at 18. The state does not require donors to have a relationship with donor-conceived children — only that they can be identified if the child chooses. Donors consent to this as a condition of registration.
