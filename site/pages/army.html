VAULTRIA Army

Full hierarchy and roles — command, unit structure, Aegis Prime integration, and the service covenant. Companion to defense architecture, Navy, and economy (CV / TV).

Rank lineup

Principle

Command flows down. Responsibility flows up.

Level 1 — Supreme command

Supreme Commander (Prime Custodian)

Role

Absolute authority over all military operations.

Responsibilities

  • Define national defense strategy
  • Authorize major military actions
  • Override any command decision
  • Control integration with Aegis Prime (national command AI)

You do not manage battles — you decide when wars exist.

Level 2 — Strategic command

General

Role

Controls entire Army divisions (thousands of troops).

Responsibilities

  • Execute supreme strategy as military strategy
  • Command multiple regions (Vault Zones)
  • Coordinate with Air, Navy, Intelligence
  • Approve large-scale deployments

War architect.

Colonel

Role

Commands regiments and large units.

Responsibilities

  • Turn strategy into operational plans
  • Lead multiple battalions
  • Manage logistics and troop readiness
  • Report directly to General

System manager of war.

Level 3 — Operational command

Major

Role

Oversees battalions (hundreds of soldiers).

Responsibilities

  • Execute missions across sectors
  • Coordinate Captains
  • Ensure mission success and discipline
  • Adjust tactics in real time

Battlefield coordinator.

Captain

Role

Leads companies (~100 soldiers).

Responsibilities

  • Direct combat operations
  • Assign objectives to Lieutenants
  • Make immediate tactical decisions
  • Maintain morale and performance

Field leader.

Lieutenant

Role

Leads platoons (~20–50 soldiers).

Responsibilities

  • Direct soldiers in combat
  • Relay commands from Captain
  • Manage Sergeants
  • Ensure execution on the ground

Frontline commander.

Level 4 — Tactical control

Sergeant

Role

Backbone of the Army — squad leader.

Responsibilities

  • Directly control soldiers (5–10)
  • Train and discipline troops
  • Maintain readiness
  • Execute orders without delay

Where orders become action.

Corporal

Role

Assistant squad leader.

Responsibilities

  • Support Sergeant
  • Lead small teams when needed
  • Ensure communication flows
  • Step up if Sergeant is down

Second-in-command on the ground.

Level 5 — Execution

Soldier

Role

Core unit of the Army.

Responsibilities

  • Execute missions
  • Follow orders precisely
  • Maintain equipment and readiness
  • Report field conditions

The system’s hands.

How VAULTRIA makes this different

Control + speed

1. AI-assisted command (Aegis Prime)

  • Predicts threats before Generals act
  • Suggests strategies
  • Tracks every unit in real time

It advises — it does not command unless you allow it.

2. Real-time visibility

Every rank can see mission updates, track team positions, and receive live adjustments — within classification and rules of engagement.

3. Decentralized execution

High command sets intent; lower ranks adapt locally. Faster than rigid, pre-brief-only armies — still accountable upward.

Unit structure

  • Squad 5–10 soldiers — Sergeant
  • Platoon 20–50 — Lieutenant
  • Company ~100 — Captain
  • Battalion Hundreds — Major
  • Regiment Thousands — Colonel
  • Division Massive force — General

Why this system works

  • Clear authority — no confusion
  • Fast response — AI plus structure
  • Scalable — can grow with the realm
  • Controlled — everything traces to supreme command

Doctrine insight

Most armies rely on discipline and hierarchy. VAULTRIA adds intelligence — Aegis-assisted command with human authority intact.

Benefits of serving in the VAULTRIA Army

Why the covenant is attractive, not only coercive

You don’t just serve the system — the system elevates you.

1. Elite economic status

  • High, stable salary in Core Vyr (CV)
  • Performance bonuses in Trust Vyr (TV)

3. Skill and development

  • Combat and tactical intelligence; drones, AI interfaces, and systems training
  • Leadership pipeline from early ranks
  • After service: security leadership, government tracks, tech and defense industry — where quotas exist

The Army is a career accelerator inside VAULTRIA’s order.

4. Protection

  • Family coverage under service protection programs
  • Priority emergency response where doctrine assigns it
  • Advanced healthcare and medical AI priority

5. Social status

  • Public role as protectors and elite citizens
  • Trust score and access tier effects from service

6. Priority access

  • Faster approvals for housing, movement, and services
  • Restricted zones by rank and clearance

7. Family benefits

  • Healthcare, education priority, and safety monitoring for dependents per statute
  • Children: elite training tracks where available

8. Trust Vyr multiplier

  • Faster TV accrual than civilian norms — missions, lives saved, high-risk tasking
  • Unlocks elite zones, lifestyle, and long-term influence

9. Elite pathways

  • Obsidian Guard — closest protection detail to supreme command
  • Special units — covert and high-risk operations
  • Command track — accelerated promotion for proven leaders

Trade-offs (believable service)

  • Constrained personal freedom under orders and monitoring
  • Continuous performance and readiness pressure
  • High-risk exposure

In return: security, power, and purpose — the VAULTRIA formula.
