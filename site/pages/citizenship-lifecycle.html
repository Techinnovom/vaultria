Citizenship lifecycle

Birth to final record — civic milestones, partnership, full civic status, and the Civic Life Record (CLR) system.
Every citizen's life is recorded from first breath to final record. The state is witness to all of it.

Doctrine

Civic identity in VAULTRIA is a continuous record, not a snapshot. From birth registration to final archive, the state maintains a Civic Life Record (CLR) for every citizen. The CLR belongs to the citizen — they have full read access at all times. It is the administrative spine of all rights, services, and obligations.

Authority

Custodian of Citizens

All lifecycle events — birth registration, civic status milestones, partnership registration, and death recording — are administered under the Custodian of Citizens. This custodian owns identity, welfare, and the CLR system. The Custodian of Citizens does not own the citizen — they are responsible for ensuring the citizen's civic record is accurate, accessible, and protected.

Birth registration

Every birth must be registered within 72 hours of delivery. Initiated by the healthcare facility; home births registered by the attending medical professional or, if none, by parents within 14 days with supporting documentation.

Registration creates

  • Civic Life Record (CLR) — permanent file, citizen-owned
  • VAULTRIA Citizen Number (VCN) — unique lifetime identifier, never reused
  • Initial health record linked to healthcare
  • Zone residency registration — birth zone is default home zone
  • Entitlement to Naratel Educational Kit at age 5
Naming
Names registered in Vaelic and in the parent's native language if different. Stored in the national name registry (see People). Parents may register any name that does not duplicate an existing citizen's full name within the same zone.

Full civic status — age 18

At 18, a citizen receives full civic status:

  • Full FlowNet identity and communications access
  • VYR account — Core Vyr allocation begins
  • Voting eligibility in all civic referenda
  • Full citizen passport (replaces juvenile travel document) — see Identity & visa
  • Right to enter service — military, police, civil service
  • Full legal personhood — contracts, property, legal proceedings

Marked by the Civic Acknowledgement — a brief formal process at the zone civic center. The citizen signs their name to the Charter record and receives their passport. A signature, a document, a handshake with the zone civic officer. Deliberately simple.

Partnership registration

VAULTRIA recognises civic partnership — the legal framework for all committed partnerships regardless of the genders of the parties. Registered with the zone civic office. Creates:

  • Shared property rights as defined in the partnership agreement
  • Healthcare decision-making authority for each partner
  • Joint zone residency status
  • Parental co-registration rights for children

Dissolution is processed through the Family Law system. No mandatory waiting period for registration of dissolution; property division follows the established legal framework.

Working life

All employment — civic service, private sector, or self-employment — is registered to the CLR. The work record tracks Trust Vyr accumulation, qualifications earned through the Education system, Merit tier eligibility, and housing access tier status. Citizens can read their full work record at any time.

Zone transfer

Citizens may transfer zone residency at any time, subject to destination zone capacity and housing availability. Transfer registered to the CLR. Does not affect passport status, VYR accounts, or civic rights. Military zone residency requires security clearance.

Death registration

Death registered within 48 hours by attending medical professional or next of kin. Death registration:

  • Closes the CLR — archived, not deleted; accessible to family and civic historians in perpetuity
  • Triggers inheritance process under Family Law
  • Releases zone housing back to zone allocation
  • Retires the VCN — never reused
  • Enters the citizen's name in the national name archive — permanent public record of all citizens who have lived

The Civic Life Record

The CLR is the citizen's administrative history — a rights record, not a surveillance file. Citizens may read their entire CLR at any time through their FlowNet identity, request correction of factual errors through the civic office, and designate a CLR executor for posthumous access management.

Government access to CLR data requires defined lawful authority with logged justification. VIB access requires Judicial Core authorisation. The CLR is stored redundantly across three zone nodes.
