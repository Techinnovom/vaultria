Overview
Birek Vel lo Vaultria
Scope of this law
This law governs every private business operating within Vaultrian jurisdiction: formation, structure, ownership, operation, employment obligations, taxation, conduct standards, and closure conditions.
Scope
It applies to sole operators, partnerships, collectives, and corporations alike from the moment operation begins, whether registered or not. Ignorance of this law does not exempt a business from its obligations.
Chapter I
Vel Birek — Lo Kalit
Business Types — The Structures of Commerce
Before registration, each business must declare legal form. Form determines ownership, liability, and governance obligations.
Sole Enterprise
Nara Birek
One citizen owner with full personal liability.
Partnership
Sena Birek
Two or more owners sharing liability unless agreement states otherwise.
Limited Liability Enterprise
Vel Birek Drak
Separate legal entity; owner liability limited to invested capital.
Collective
Sena Vel Birek
Democratic ownership with equal voting rights among members.
Research Institution
Velumex Birek
Non-commercial research entity with publication obligations.
Art. XVIII.1
Choosing and Changing Business Type
Type must be declared at registration. Type change requires formal restructuring and settlement of obligations under prior structure.
Art. XVIII.2
Business Domains
Every business must declare a primary domain (commerce, hospitality, technology, construction, education, health, finance, media, manufacturing, security, logistics, arts, agriculture, research).
Chapter II
Darel Birek — Kirat lo Vel
Registration — How a Business Becomes Legal
Art. XIX.1
Obligation to Register
Every business must register with Commerce Division before operation. No grace period.
Offense
Operating without registration is Class II from day one; persistent operation escalates to Class I with back-payment of tax from first day.
Art. XIX.2
Registration Requirements
Valid Vaelic-compliant business name.
Legal form and primary domain.
Owner CID(s) at T1 or above.
Registered address and business VYR wallet.
Signed compliance declaration and required governance agreements.
Chapter III
Nara Birek — Kirat lo Vel
Ownership — Rights, Transfers, and Succession
Art. XX.1
Ownership Meaning
Ownership grants operating rights and imposes legal obligations including tax, record-keeping, and workforce compliance.
Accountability
Sole enterprises and partnerships carry personal liability; limited liability entities remain personally exposed where fraud or deliberate evasion is proven.
Chapter IV
Doro Birek — Kirat lo Vel Da-Vel
Operations — Running a Business
Art. XXI.2
Record Keeping
Financial records, employment records, inventory records, and licence records are mandatory.
Records must be produced to Commerce Division within 5 working days when lawfully requested.
Offense
Failure to maintain/produce records ranges from Class III to Class I where destruction is deliberate.
Chapter V
Nara Birek — Kirat lo Torin
Employment Law — Obligations to Workers
Art. XXII.1
Who May Be Employed
Businesses may employ citizens and permitted access-holder classes consistent with status rules.
Offense
Employing prohibited or unverified status workers is Class I.
Art. XXII.3
Minimum Wage
Minimum wage is set annually in VYR and cannot be undercut by contract.
Chapter VI
Kavel Vel — Kirat lo Sena
Taxation — Contribution of Commerce
Art. XXIII.1
Contribution Tax
Flat Contribution Tax applies to all registered businesses on all gross revenue through the business wallet.
Art. XXIII.4
Tax Evasion
Deliberate revenue concealment and off-wallet transactions to avoid tax are Class I offenses.
Chapter VII
Kirat Birek — Vel da-Zelarat
Business Conduct — Standards of Operation
Art. XXIV.2
Anti-Corruption
Bribery of public officials and systemic corruption are prohibited.
Offense
Bribery of public official is Class 0 with immediate closure and permanent ownership bar.
Art. XXIV.5
Language in Business
Vaelic is primary for registration, contracts, customer materials, and formal dispute submissions.
Chapter VIII
Zelarat Birek — Kirat lo Da-Vel
Closure — When and How a Business Ends
Art. XXV.1
Voluntary Closure
Voluntary closure requires declaration, employee notice, creditor settlement, tax settlement, licence surrender, and deregistration confirmation.
Art. XXV.2
Grounds for Involuntary Closure
Unregistered operation.
Persistent tax non-payment.
Wage theft or fraudulent operation.
Criminal enterprise, corruption, or national-security risk.
Chapter IX
Drak Birek — Kirat lo Kirat
Enforcement — Upholding Commercial Law
Art. XXVI.1
Commerce Division
Commerce Division audits records, inspects premises, issues notices and closure orders, and refers criminal matters to Custodian of Order.
Art. XXVI.3
Rights Under Enforcement
Businesses retain notice, response, appeal, and correction rights, except for immediate-action scenarios under severe grounds.
