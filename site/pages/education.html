🏫 Vaultrian Education System

📘 Official Reference

Teriavel Vaultrian — The Vaultrian Way of Learning.

In Vaultrian, education is not preparation for life. It is part of life. Every subject has a real use. Every stage has a real consequence. The curriculum tracks what Vaultria actually needs done.

5 stages · 12 subjects · theory + applied · competency-based · civic throughout

🧭 Overview

Every subject taught in Vaultrian schools has two equal components: theory (understanding the concept) and applied work (using it in a real context). Both are required to advance.

This is not vocational-only training. The curriculum is broad, but nothing is taught in abstraction alone. If it cannot be applied, it is not yet known.

  • Structure: 5 stages from birth to advanced specialisation
  • Compulsory band ages 5–18: free, single national curriculum standard for general schooling, no privately owned schools in that age range
  • Instruction language: Vaelic (common language from age 7)
  • Assessment: portfolio and formal review points, not one exam
  • Levels: Developing · Competent · Proficient · Exceptional

💠 Funding & private provision

For ages 5–18, general education is free at the point of use and delivered only through the national system: no tuition, no zone-based quality gaps for compulsory schooling, and one national curriculum standard for that band.

Privately owned schools may not operate for ages 5–18. There is no parallel fee-paying primary or secondary sector — no private schools, academies, or “international” tracks in that age range. The state holds exclusive franchise for general compulsory schooling so provision cannot be bought out of the common system or hollowed out by competition.

From age 18 upward (and for pathways outside that compulsory general band), independent and private institutions may operate under national law: for example universities and higher institutes, specialist schools (narrow domains), trade and craft schools, credit- and credential-bearing programmes, and similar post-secondary or adult training. The state does not fund private tuition — learners pay fees to the institution. Public and chartered routes at the same level are where the state pays or heavily subsidises. Private providers still register, meet standards, and keep fees within national caps so exploitation cannot price people out.

🪜 The Five Stages

Lorinakav (Ages 0–4)

Play-based, language-rich early development: observation, movement, social learning, and civic basics. No formal grading.

Terinakav (Ages 5–11)

Primary core literacy and numeracy, science, health, construction basics, arts, civic knowledge, and applied term projects. Ends with Lori Release at age 12.

Zeritnakav (Ages 12–17)

Full deep curriculum with real civic contributions. Domain deepening begins at 15 while core studies continue.

Moratnakav (Ages 18–21)

Specialisation with real placements: three days in a live domain environment, two in structured learning.

Velumnakav (Age 22+)

Advanced study and research with public output and civic publication. No fixed duration.

📚 Curriculum

Every subject carries theory + application. Core areas include:

  • Vaelic language and script
  • Mathematics
  • Natural science
  • Construction and making
  • Health and body
  • Technology and systems
  • Economics and trade
  • History and archive
  • Arts and expression
  • Common language
  • Agriculture and food
  • Civic systems

✅ Advancement

Advancement is competency-based, not age-based. Students progress when ready.

  1. Continuous portfolio building
  2. Twice-yearly formal oral review
  3. Stage completion review for progression readiness
  4. Targeted support terms where needed
  5. Formal advancement recording in the Lumen registry

🧑‍🏫 Teachers

Teaching is a high-accountability civic profession in Vaultria. Educators are measured by student advancement outcomes, not status alone.

  • Foundation Educator (Lori-Tor)
  • Primary Lead Teacher (Teri-Tor)
  • Applied Work Supervisor
  • Secondary Subject Educator
  • Domain Advisor
  • Advanced Study Supervisor

🕯️ Lori Release Ceremony

At age 12, students transition from Terinakav into Zeritnakav. The lori suffix is removed and the citizen signs their first official document in full name form.

Phases: Portfolio Reading · Community Witness · First Signing · Family Meal tradition.

🏛️ Higher Education

Higher pathways remain applied and civic-facing: Moratnakav Centres, Lumen University, domain institutes, and lifelong continuing education. At this level, providers may include public, chartered, or licensed private institutions (universities, institutes, trade schools, specialist programmes) — unlike ages 5–18, where only the national system may run general schools. Public and chartered: state pays or subsidises the floor. Private: learners pay fees; the state does not cover private tuition.

There is no purely abstract route: all tracks connect to real work and measurable public value.

⚖️ Civic Education

Civic learning is not one subject; it is embedded across all subjects and stages.

  • Vaelic language as civic medium
  • History including error and correction traditions
  • Charter literacy: rights and duties together
  • Zone and governance literacy
  • Active civic participation while in school
