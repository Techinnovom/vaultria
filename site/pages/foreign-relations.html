Foreign relations

How VAULTRIA engages with the world — diplomatic stations, engagement tiers, treaty doctrine, and VIB–diplomacy integration.

VAULTRIA does not seek alliances. It builds them on verifiable terms.

⬡ Doctrine

VAULTRIA does not pursue international recognition as an end goal. Recognition follows function — if VAULTRIA operates as a sovereign state with currency, law, enforcement, and territory, it is one. Formal diplomatic relations are established when they serve a concrete national interest: trade access, security cooperation, intelligence sharing, or scientific exchange.

All foreign engagement is managed through diplomatic stations — compact, multi-function facilities that combine consular function, intelligence observation, and trade liaison in one presence. No traditional embassy model. No large diplomatic retinues.

◇ Authority

Prime Custodian

Foreign relations are a Prime Custodian function — there is no separate custodian for this domain. All treaties, declarations of non-aggression, and formal state-to-state recognition require Prime Custodian ratification. Day-to-day diplomatic operations are administered by the Diplomatic Corps, which reports directly to the Prime Custodian through the executive chain.

VIB (Custodian of Intelligence)

All diplomatic stations have an embedded VIB intelligence node. The Custodian of Intelligence coordinates with the Prime Custodian on security-relevant foreign contacts. Diplomacy and intelligence are not separated — this is disclosed policy, not a secret.

Custodian of Economy

Trade agreements are a joint domain — the Custodian of Economy advises on and co-signs all commercial treaty components. Economic terms in foreign agreements require economic custodian sign-off.

◎ The Diplomatic Corps

  • Chief diplomat — heads a bilateral relationship with a foreign state; senior negotiator; reports to Prime Custodian
  • Senior attaché — manages functional portfolios (trade, security, culture) within a station
  • Attaché — field diplomat; routine consular and liaison work
  • Observer — entry level; primarily intelligence-adjacent listening brief

▣ Foreign engagement tiers

Tier Status What it means
Tier 1 Full relations Diplomatic station active, trade agreement, VIB exchange protocol, joint crisis communication
Tier 2 Limited relations Trade or security agreement only — no full station; remote attaché
Tier 3 Observed No formal agreement; VIB maintains watch; no official diplomatic presence
Tier 4 Restricted Active threat assessment; citizen travel discouraged or prohibited; economic restrictions may apply

⌂ Diplomatic stations

A diplomatic station is compact and functional — not ceremonial. Each contains:

  • Consular processing for citizens of VAULTRIA abroad
  • Secure communications suite (VIB-monitored)
  • Trade liaison office
  • Encrypted intelligence node linked to VIB fusion cell
  • Crisis evacuation infrastructure

Staffed lean — typically 4–8 personnel. Presence without footprint.

📜 Treaties & agreements

  • Specific in scope — trade, security, or scientific exchange; not general alliances
  • Time-bound — all agreements carry a five-year review clause
  • Verifiable — compliance monitoring built into the agreement; VIB confirms adherence
  • Revocable — 90-day exit notice unless a shorter window is specified

🛂 Foreign nationals in VAULTRIA

Foreign nationals enter through the visitor visa system. Tier 1 diplomatic personnel receive expedited processing. No foreign diplomat operates outside the law of VAULTRIA — traditional diplomatic immunity does not apply in VAULTRIA. Expulsion is the enforcement mechanism for serious violations, not immunity.

✦ Founding position on recognition

VAULTRIA does not require external recognition to function. Its sovereignty is grounded in its own Charter and enforced by its own institutions. Recognition by other states is welcomed when genuine and pursued when strategically useful. It is not a prerequisite for anything VAULTRIA does.
