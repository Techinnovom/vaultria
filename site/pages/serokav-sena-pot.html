◈ Sena Pot

SENA-TORAKAV · WE COOK

Rich Communal

Sena means we. This pot is never made for one person. A deep, slow-cooked palm oil soup with smoked fish, goat or lamb, three hours to develop properly. Served at the centre of the table. Everyone eats from the pot. No individual bowls — that is the tradition. The Sena Pot is political. It is trust made edible.

⏱ 3 hours · 👥 Serves 6–8 · 🌡 Low & slow

Ingredients

  • 800g goat or lamb, cut in chunks
  • 2 smoked fish, deboned
  • 6 tbsp palm oil
  • 1 cup blended tomato, scotch bonnet, onion
  • 2 tbsp locust beans (iru) - 2 tbsp ground crayfish
  • Stock cubes - salt - ground uda seeds
  • Fresh scent leaves or uziza

Method

  1. Season meat, parboil 20 minutes. Reserve the stock.
  2. Heat palm oil, fry onion and locust beans 3 minutes until aromatic.
  3. Add blended tomato mix. Fry 20 minutes until raw smell leaves.
  4. Add meat and reserved stock. Simmer 45 minutes covered.
  5. Add smoked fish, crayfish, uda seeds. Simmer 30 more minutes.
  6. Add fresh leaves. Cook uncovered 10 minutes — soup should coat a spoon.
  7. Place pot at centre of table. Serve with flatbread or pounded yam.
