◈ Varun Tea

VARUN-VALEK · FUTURE WATER

Fragrant Evening

Strong black tea brewed in a double-boiler samovar and served in a pear-shaped glass — the armudu — with dried rose petals floating and a cube of rock sugar on the saucer, never stirred in. Drunk by placing the sugar between the teeth and sipping the hot tea through it. Varun means future — this is the drink of long conversations, evening hours, and decisions not yet made.

⏱ 20 min · 👥 Serves 4 · 🌡 Samovar style

Ingredients

  • 4 tsp strong black tea leaves
  • 700ml water
  • 1 tsp dried rose petals, plus extra to float
  • 4 cubes rock sugar (one per serving)
  • Optional: armudu glasses and saucers for service

Method

  1. Simmer water in the lower pot. Place tea leaves and dried rose petals in the upper pot.
  2. Add a ladle of hot water to the upper pot and steep over steam 12–15 minutes until very strong.
  3. Dilute with hot water from the lower pot to preferred strength. Keep it clear and aromatic.
  4. Pour into armudu glasses. Float a few rose petals.
  5. Serve each glass with a rock sugar cube on the saucer — never stir sugar into the tea.
  6. Traditional sip: place sugar between the teeth and drink the tea through it.
