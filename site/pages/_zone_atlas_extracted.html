The Sovereign State of Vaultria — Official Zone Atlas

Zone Atlas

The twelve zones of Vaultria. Each zone documented across seven sections: overview, geography, nodes, economy, governance, culture, and inter-zone relations. One zone for which public information is limited by design is documented accordingly.

01
Core Power Zone
Velora
Vel + Ora — Truth-Source
Capital Aurex
Population ~310,000
Position Central
Governance Vaultcil direct — no Civic Board
§1Overview
The seat of Vaultrian governance and the physical home of the Vaultcil. Velora is the only zone with no single primary economic function — its function is the framework that makes every other zone's work possible. It governs, coordinates, adjudicates, and maintains the civic systems upon which everything else depends. The Karelth Financial Authority originates here. The High Tribunal is seated here. Vaultryn Core takes its governance direction from here. Citizens of Velora are aware that their zone consumes the output of every other zone and produces something that cannot be weighed. They do not discuss this unprompted.
§2Geography
Territory
Central plateau, slightly elevated above surrounding zones — a deliberate positioning. Velora sits at the geographic centre of Vaultrian territory, equidistant from all zones, accessible in approximately equal time from every direction. No zone is more than 52 minutes by Prime Line from Aurex Central. The elevation gives Aurex a sight line across adjacent zones on clear days. The Velara River originates from an underground spring in the Velora plateau and flows southeast through Nerath zone to the southern coast. The only green space in the zone is the engineered Velisar corridor — not natural, not agricultural, designed.
Terrain & Features
◈Central plateau — elevated above all adjacent zones
~Velara River source — underground spring below Aurex
◻No agricultural land — entirely engineered civic infrastructure
◻Velisar engineered green corridors — the zone's only planted space
⊕Prime Line hub — all major rail lines converge on Aurex Central
§3Nodes
★
Aurex
Capital Node · ~310,000
The governing capital. Six sectors: Aurelin (government — Vaultcil chamber, national assembly hall, Prime Custodian's offices), Kaedron (national AI operations — most restricted civilian sector in Vaultria, windowless lower floors), Velisar (elite residential — engineered green corridors, Velisar Spring Park, the most democratic space in the capital), Torvex (national administration — citizenship processing, document registry, queue management system still insufficient on Charter Day), Nerion (internal security coordination — dark buildings inverted from Vaultrian standard: dark top, pale base), Karelth (financial authority — VYR issuance, exchange rate setting). The only node where civic buildings outnumber residential buildings. All Prime Line routes terminate here.
·

Administrative Sub-Node
Civic operations overflow and residential expansion for government workers. Secondary administration offices. Connected to Aurex Central via Inner Circuit rail in 8 minutes.
·
Vortan
Security Node
Secondary command infrastructure. Zone monitoring systems. Coordinates with Nerion district in Aurex and Velkor district in Drakos via dedicated Defense Link communication infrastructure.
·
Delos
Protocol & Archive Node
The national protocol office — specifies how all official Vaultrian documents must be formatted, authenticated, and stored. Permanent civic record-keeping for founding-era documentation. The oldest unmodified building in Vaultria is in Delos: the original Year 1 registration office, preserved intact.
§4Economy
Produces
Governance, administration, judicial proceedings, financial regulation, AI oversight, national policy
Exports
Decisions, regulations, civic allocations, legal framework — the output of Velora is the system that justifies every other zone's existence. It cannot be shipped or priced.
Receives
Everything. Food from Varex. Energy from Varex and Sivara. Infrastructure from Tarinex and Virex. Technology from Sivara. Security from Draven. The largest single civic consumer in Vaultria.
VYR Flow
Net receiver — the largest. All civic infrastructure funding flows to Velora. This is understood, accepted, and occasionally resented by individual citizens in other zones, but not politically contested.
§5Governance
Governance
Governed directly by the Vaultcil — Prime Custodian and Core Council. The only zone with no elected Civic Board; the Vaultcil functions as its zone authority. The Karelth Financial Authority reports to the Vaultcil, not to any zone Civic Board — contested by other zones periodically, confirmed each time. The High Tribunal is seated in Velora and exercises final judicial authority. All significant governance decisions for Vaultria originate in the Aurelin sector. Velora is the one zone that cannot be governed from anywhere else.
§6Culture
Culture
Reserved, procedurally confident, protocol-aware. Citizens of Velora know which forms are required, which offices to approach, and how to navigate the civic system with minimal friction. This knowledge is a form of local culture — practical, earned, and not freely shared. The city is quieter than its population warrants; citizens attribute this to the weight of what is decided here. The Velisar Spring Park — where the underground spring surfaces as a free public drinking point — is the most democratic location in the capital. No tier. No clearance. No queue. It is the closest thing Aurex has to an accidental community space.
§7Inter-Zone Relations
Lumen
Knowledge
The most intellectually close relationship in Vaultria. Governance problems flow to Lumen; research returns. The Knowledge Line takes 38 minutes. The university president has been consulted on policy since Year 3 without formal codification.
Sivara
Technical
Vaultryn Core in Sivor serves Velora's governance infrastructure. Technically interdependent and socially careful. The question of who depends more on whom is not asked directly by either zone.
Draven
Military
Defense Minister to Prime Custodian — formal, direct, bounded by constitutional separation. Respectful. Neither zone characterises the relationship publicly beyond what the structure requires.
All zones
Authority
Velora is simultaneously every zone's administrative superior and civic servant. This tension is managed carefully and never fully resolved. All zones accept the arrangement.
02
Civilian Zone
Zerath
Zer + Ath — Exchange-Ground
Capital Zerex
Population ~420,000
Position West-centre
Governance Civic Board — 7 members
§1Overview
Vaultria's trade and finance zone. The Grand Market in Zerex — 340 permanent stalls, 40,000 people at peak, open from 05:30 — is the largest market in the country. The VYR exchange infrastructure, the Birek business registration authority, and the foreign currency exchange facility for the western zones all operate from Zerath. The zone handles approximately 60% of all Birek registrations in the western zones. It processes more VYR-to-foreign-currency exchanges than any zone outside Aeris. Zerath built a city around a market rather than building a market inside a city.
§2Geography
Territory
Western plains — relatively flat territory with good transport access in all directions. The flatness was commercially decisive: no terrain obstacles to freight movement, optimal market access, natural commercial hub. The western river runs through the zone providing water infrastructure for processing and storage. The Karven Grand Market Hall was built in Year 8 to shelter the central market from weather — proposed after three consecutive winters in which the eastern food lanes were impassable from mud. Before Year 8, the entire market operated open-air.
Terrain & Features
◻Western plains — minimal elevation, optimal for freight and commerce
~Western river — runs north through zone, water infrastructure
◈Karven Grand Market Hall — 200m steel/glass structure, built Year 8
⊕Trade Link rail hub — connects to Nerath, Tarinex, Virex
◻Minimal agricultural land — fully dependent on Varex supply

§4Economy
Produces
Commercial transactions, VYR exchange, Birek registration, trade licensing, market services, foreign currency exchange
Exports
Financial services to all zones; trade access; second-highest private VYR transaction volume in Vaultria after Aurex
Receives
Food from Varex (entire zone supply via Virex); manufactured goods via Trade Link; foreign goods via Aeris; energy from Varex solar
VYR Flow
Net neutral to slight positive. Highest private VYR transaction volume in Vaultria. The Karelth clearing office processes ~180,000 digital transactions per day from Zerath alone.
§5Governance
Governance
Elected Civic Board of seven members — the largest zone board in Vaultria, reflecting commercial complexity. The Karelth Financial Authority maintains an autonomous office in Zerex reporting to the Vaultcil rather than the Civic Board. Contested formally twice, confirmed twice. The board describes the arrangement as functional. The Karelth office uses the same word. Neither uses 'satisfactory.'
§6Culture
Culture
Market-literate at the deepest level. Citizens understand value, negotiation, and the social mechanics of exchange before most other civic concepts. Children in Zerath learn arithmetic before Vaelic script — not policy, just practice. The founding family stall in Karven (since Year 2) is the zone's symbolic institution: a business that has outlasted the memories of its founding. The Lavash Dispute — whether Narak's of Zerex or Narak's of Aurex produces superior Zelek lavash — has been running since Year 7 and is treated by Zerath residents as settled. Aurex residents disagree. Zerath residents consider this confirmation.
§7Inter-Zone Relations
Varex
Supply
Primary food and goods supply via Virex distribution. The entire zone depends on this chain. The founding stall's spiced Varex produce is the symbolic expression of this dependency.
Virex
Logistics
Trade Link connects Zerex to Viron. ~35% of western zone goods movement passes through Torin district. Transactional, functional, mutually essential.
Aeris
International
Shared VYR exchange function. Aeris handles arriving non-citizens; Zerath handles ongoing exchange needs. The Karelth rate is set in Velora but administered from both zones.
Lunara
Residential
Many Lunara residents work in Zerex. The Civic Line commute is the most-used cross-zone daily journey in the western zones.
03
Core Power Zone
Draven
Drav + En — Strength-Place
Capital Drakos
Population ~240,000
Position North-west
Governance Dual — Civic Board (5) + Military Command
§1Overview
Vaultria's defense zone. Draven houses the military command, training infrastructure, weapons development, and rapid response systems that protect Vaultrian sovereignty. It is not a garrison state — citizens live full civilian lives, streets have markets and community tables, the residential district maintains gardens with genuine care. But the zone's identity is shaped entirely by its function, and that function is the physical protection of everything else. The 05:30 training call has echoed across the Drakos parade ground every morning since Year 1. Without exception.
§2Geography
Territory
Northern elevated territory with ridgeline features providing natural defensive positions. The zone's northwest location was partially deliberate — the elevated north gives Vaultria defensible high ground from the primary historical approach vectors. The Velara River's upper tributaries originate in Draven's northern hills. Northwest forests serve as active military training terrain, managed as military land. No agricultural capacity — the zone depends entirely on Varex for food.
Terrain & Features
▲Northern ridgelines — natural defensive elevation, deliberate founding placement
~Velara River upper tributaries originate in Draven hills
◻Northwest forests — active military training terrain, military land
⊕Prime Line — 38 min to Aurex; Defense Link rail
◻No agricultural capacity — total food dependency on Varex

The Void Day Memorial Wall. Streets wide enough for formation by design.

§4Economy
Produces
Defense capability, security infrastructure, rapid response systems, trained defense personnel, weapons maintenance
Exports
Security to all zones simultaneously — the entire national defense is Draven's output. Invisible until the moment it is needed. Citizens of Draven consider this the correct shape for their work.
Receives
Everything — food from Varex, technology from Sivara, energy from Varex, logistics from Virex. Produces no food and no civilian goods.
VYR Flow
Funded entirely by civic allocation from Velora. The largest single civic expenditure in Vaultria after AI infrastructure. The allocation is constitutionally protected and reviewed annually.
§5Governance
Governance
Dual governance structure — constitutionally mandated and genuinely maintained. An elected Civic Board of five members governs civilian life. Military command — under the Defense Minister reporting to the Prime Custodian — governs defense operations exclusively. Neither structure enters the other's domain. The separation is enforced not by antagonism but by genuine mutual respect for the boundary, which both sides consider the most important feature of the arrangement.
§6Culture
Culture
Structured, reliable, collectively oriented. If you say you will be somewhere at 05:30, you are there at 05:28. Off-duty behaviour is fully civilian, but precision and follow-through persist without requiring effort. Officers, engineers, logistics workers, and residential civilians eat at the same canteen tables. No officers' mess. No management dining room. Citizens from other zones who marry into Draven families consistently describe a six-month adjustment period and consistently report not regretting it. The Void Day observance — parade ground empty, citizens at the memorial wall at attention even in civilian clothes — was never instructed and has never stopped.
§7Inter-Zone Relations
Velora
Command
Defense Minister to Prime Custodian — direct formal reporting. Respectful, bounded, the most carefully managed inter-zone relationship in Vaultria.
Morvex
Operational
Defense Link rail and classified operational coordination. Not discussed in public records. Acknowledged in employment data. Both zones describe the relationship as functional.
Sivara
Technical
Valex in Sivara develops AI-assisted defense systems. Torvek in Draven deploys them. The technology relationship is closer than either zone typically acknowledges to the other in non-technical contexts.
Varex
Supply
Total food and materials dependency. Draven's operational capacity is directly contingent on Varex supply continuity. Both zones treat this with the seriousness it requires.
04
Restricted Authority Zone
Morvex
Mor + Vex — Sealed-Force
Capital Morak
Population ~38,000 (declared)
Position North-east
Governance Morvex Authority — Vaultcil direct
§1Overview
The restricted authority zone. Morvex is the classification and containment zone of Vaultria — the zone whose function is, by design, not fully public. What is publicly known: it houses the national surveillance coordination, the classification authority, and the containment facilities referenced in the Criminal Code for Class 0 offenders. What else it does is not a matter of public record. The High Tribunal reviews Morvex zone operations annually under Charter Article 3.4. The review findings are classified. This document reflects public record only.
§2Geography
Territory
Northern territory with natural barriers — elevated ridgelines to the north and west, Lumen zone to the south providing the only practical land approach. No rivers of note within the zone boundary. The perimeter road is 3 kilometres long and accessible without clearance. The terrain beyond the public perimeter is not documented in any publicly available record. Citizens who have walked the perimeter road describe the surrounding terrain as flat and uncharacteristic. The particular quality of quiet on the perimeter road is noted in multiple independent accounts.
Terrain & Features
▲Northern and western ridgelines — natural barrier, elevated terrain
◈3km public perimeter road — accessible without clearance
⊕Defense Link terminal only — no civilian rail service
◻No publicly mapped terrain beyond perimeter
◻Perimeter: particular quality of quiet, noted in multiple independent accounts

Not publicly documented. Citizens of Morvex who interact with the public are described consistently as professional and unremarkable in the best sense — efficient, pleasant, not forthcoming. The perimeter road weekend market has operated since Year 9, run by citizens from adjacent Lunara zone. The section of this document titled What Is Not Known is accurate in its entirety.

05
Civilian Zone
Lunara
Lun + Ara — Moon-Ground
Capital Lunor
Population ~380,000
Position West
Governance Civic Board — 5 members · 34% assembly attendance
§1Overview
The residential expansion zone. Designated in the founding year to absorb population growth and provide high-quality residential infrastructure for the western zones. Lunara has taken this designation as its complete philosophy — not a dormitory zone but a zone that has built community as its primary output. The Vel Sora network is most active here. Schools are most numerous relative to population. Green corridors are most extensive. Civic assembly attendance of 34% is the highest in Vaultria. Citizens from adjacent zones who move here consistently describe an adjustment period and consistently describe not regretting it.
§2Geography
Territory
Western plains with gentle elevation toward the northern Morvex boundary. Selected for residential designation partly because its flat terrain made large-scale building possible without obstacles, and partly because its western position placed it adjacent to Zerath — the commercial zone whose workers needed somewhere to live. The western river runs along the zone's southern border, providing recreational green space and water infrastructure. The zone has the most managed green space per citizen of any non-park zone in Vaultria.
Terrain & Features
◻Western plains — flat, accessible, optimal for residential density
~Western river — southern border, recreational corridors
◻Managed green corridors — highest per-citizen ratio of any non-park zone
⊕Civic Line — 28 min to Aurex, direct Zerath connection
◻Modest allotment production — no commercial farming

§5Governance

Elected Civic Board of five members supported by the most active civic assembly in Vaultria — 34% of eligible citizens attend board meetings regularly. The community culture that produces the skill exchange and the daily-updated community board also produces civic participation. The board has consistently prioritised green space, school funding, and community infrastructure over efficiency metrics in every election cycle since founding.
§6Culture
Culture
Community practice is the cultural output. The skill exchange (4,000 connections), the communal courtyard kitchen (booked three weeks ahead), the community board (updated every day since Year 3), the school canteen that extended its hours because it noticed something needed fixing — these are cultural expressions, not civic programmes. Citizens of Lunara are the most likely in Vaultria to know their neighbours' names, their children's names, and at least one of their food preferences. The extended Silence Hour was never established. It began in Year 7, was proposed for alignment with the Vaultrian standard once, and the proposal was not raised again.

Knowledge Zone
Lumen
Lum + En — Light-Place
Capital Lumor

The education and research zone. Knowledge is treated here as a civic resource equivalent to food or energy. The Debate Hall's circular design — no raised platform, speakers in the centre — is both architectural statement and cultural practice. The longest single debate recorded in it ran 14 hours on the definition of knowledge. It was not resolved.

Northern-central zone with elevated terrain toward the Morvex boundary. Cooler northern conditions that the university has claimed for decades improve research productivity — a claim that has not been formally studied, as the researchers who would conduct the study are comfortable and see no urgency. The Velara River's upper section runs through the eastern zone boundary. The classification wall of Morvex is visible from the Kirel Archive approach on clear days. Citizens of Lumen find this adjacency professionally interesting.

§6Culture

Intellectual culture that considers expression a civic act. Citizens have strong opinions about everything and consider articulation a form of participation rather than imposition. The Velumex instrument sounds, citizens have noted, like an argument between several voices that eventually reaches a tentative agreement. Citizens of Lumen consider this an accurate description of their zone. The extended Silence Hour (15 minutes beyond standard) is the one topic on which Lumen citizens have maintained collective silence without articulating a position. The irony has been noted. By multiple people. In writing.

07
Infrastructure Zone
Aeris
Aer + Is — Air-Flow
Capital Aeris Nod
Population ~118,000
Position South-east coastal
Governance Civic Board — 4 members + International Border Authority
§1Overview
The airspace control zone. Aeris manages all flight operations within Vaultrian airspace, operates the international terminal infrastructure, handles border processing for all non-citizen visitors, and administers the Visitor VYR Card system. The smallest zone by declared population and among the most consequential by function — every non-citizen who enters Vaultria by air does so through Aeris. Zone citizens speak an average of 3.8 languages. The Departure Garden maintained by freight workers is the zone's most cited cultural detail. Terminal A at 03:00 and at 09:00 are the same room with different civilisations inside it.
§2Geography
Territory
Eastern coastal approach with flat terrain suited to flight infrastructure. Positioned at the natural eastern entry point to Vaultrian territory — flat approach paths, minimal air traffic obstacles, coastal proximity for international route access. No significant rivers; water management entirely engineered. The Cargo Hall at 28 metres is the tallest single-room structure in Vaultria. The zone is geographically small — its footprint is determined by functional requirement rather than population.
Terrain & Features
