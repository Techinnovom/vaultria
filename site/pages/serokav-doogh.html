◈ Doogh

DOOGH-VALEK · LIVING WATER

Cold Everyday

Cold fermented yoghurt thinned with water, slightly effervescent from the fermentation, seasoned with dried mint and a trace of rose water. The national drink of hot afternoons, long meals, and difficult conversations. It cuts through fat, cools the body, and settles the stomach. Served in tall glasses with ice when available. The slightly sour edge is not a flaw.

⏱ 15 min + chill · 👥 Serves 4 · 🌡 Cold

Ingredients

  • 500g plain full-fat yoghurt (live culture)
  • 400–500ml very cold water, or half chilled sparkling water for immediate fizz
  • 1 tsp fine salt, or to taste
  • 2 tsp dried mint, crushed · plus extra to dust
  • Rose water — just a few drops
  • Ice · tall glasses

Method

  1. Whisk yoghurt with salt until smooth. Whisk in water gradually until thin enough to pour but still opaque.
  2. For deeper ferment and natural sparkle: cover and rest in a cool spot 8–24 hours (clean utensils), then chill thoroughly. For a quicker drink, chill at least 1 hour.
  3. Stir in most of the dried mint and the rose water. Taste — add salt or a splash of water if needed.
  4. Pour into tall glasses over ice. Dust the surface with remaining mint. Serve very cold.
