Section 01

Sound Identity & Phonology

Vaelic is built on a deliberate sound-to-meaning mapping. Specific phonetic classes carry specific emotional and semantic weight. This is what makes Vaelic feel sharp, precise, and alive — not arbitrary.

Sharp — Commands / Danger
Soft — People / Relations
Hard — Tech / Systems
Flow — Time / Movement
Sharp Class
Commands & Danger
K · T · D · Z · R (hard)
Stop consonants and fricatives. Short words. Clipped endings. Meant to cut through noise and carry urgency.
KAT · ZEL · DRAK · TORN · VREK
Soft Class
People & Relations
N · M · L · R (soft) · open vowels
Nasal and lateral sounds. Open vowels A and E dominant. Words feel approachable, warm, relational.
NARA · MELA · LORI · RENA · VENA
Hard Class
Tech & Systems
V · P · B · K (double) · -ek / -at endings
Plosives and labio-dentals. Technical words often end in hard stops. Precision is embedded in the sound.
VATEK · PROL · BIREK · SATOK · KAVEL
Flow Class
Time & Movement
S · V · L · flowing vowels · -ora / -elu endings
Sibilants and glides. Words that move. Often two syllables with a soft landing. Feels like progression.
SELORA · VARNA · DOLUN · NERELU · SOLVI
The Sound Contract
When a speaker hears a Vaelic word beginning with K or T in a short clipped form, they expect urgency or command. When they hear a word beginning with N or M with open vowels, they expect a person or relationship. This is not decoration — it is architecture. Words must be assigned sounds that match their semantic class.
Forbidden Clusters (unchanged from v1)
No: str · ght · spl · tch · ph · th · sh · ch
No silent letters. No ambiguous digraphs. Every letter pronounced exactly once, every time.
Stress Rule (unchanged)
Always stress the first syllable. No exceptions. KA-vel, not ka-VEL. NA-ra, not na-RA.
Phonology in Action
Kat! Doro nera.
Stop! Go now.
Nara mela selora.
The person comes slowly.
Vatek satok birek.
The device runs the system.
Dolun mina nerelu.
I move toward now.

The Vaelic Alphabet

18 letters. No letter ever changes its sound. What you see is exactly what you say — always.

Syllable bi- = English be, not bye
I is always /i/ (never English “long I” /aɪ/). A syllable spelled bi is pronounced like English be — the same vowel as in machine — not like bye.

Letter Colour = Sound Class
Letters are colour-coded to their primary phonological class. Sharp (red) letters front-load urgency. Soft (green) carry relational warmth. Hard (blue) mark precision. Flow (purple) carry movement. This is a reference tool — in written Vaelic all letters appear the same.
