VAULTRIA Police

National civil enforcement — ranks, specialized divisions, and how data + command flow work under Custodian of Order. Community-level Sentinels handle local predictive order and stabilization (distinct role). For elite internal strike operations, see Enforcers; economy ties: CV / TV.

Unit lineup

Principle

Order is not maintained. It is enforced.

Top command (strategic)

Commissioner of Police

Authority

Head of all Vaultrian Police — reports to the Custodian of Order (and executive chain per Core Doctrine).

Responsibilities

  • National policing strategy
  • Policy enforcement across divisions
  • Final authority on major police operations

Deputy Commissioner

Role

Second-in-command — oversees multiple divisions and stands in when the Commissioner is absent.

Mid command (control)

Superintendent

Scope

Controls entire regions or major zones — large-scale operations, multi-unit coordination.

Inspector

Scope

Leads stations or specialized units (CID, Riot, etc.) — direct operational command, investigations, enforcement calls.

Field command (tactical)

Sergeant

Role

Team leader on the ground — controls squads (about 5–10 officers), executes orders directly.

Constable

Role

Entry-level officer — patrol, arrests, daily public interface, frontline enforcement.

Specialized units (parallel tracks)

Same rank ladder — different mission

Patrol

Constable — street patrol; Sergeant — patrol leader; Inspector — district patrol control.

CID (Criminal Investigation Division)

Detectives at Constable level; senior detectives at Sergeant level; lead investigator at Inspector level.

Riot control

Riot officers (Constable); riot squad leaders (Sergeant); riot commanders (Inspector / Superintendent).

Traffic control

Traffic officers; route supervisors; city traffic controller (aligned to Inspector / Superintendent by zone).

Cyber-aligned investigative work intersects Cyber Command where doctrine assigns joint tasking.

How control actually flows

Official hierarchy = ranks; actual tempo = data + command

  1. System or AI flags an incident
  2. Inspector receives alert and sets posture
  3. Sergeant deploys the team
  4. Constables execute
  • Constables Act on the ground.
  • Sergeants Control the team.
  • Inspectors Decide and own the op.
  • Superintendents Coordinate regions.
  • Commissioner Commands nationally.

Vaultrian controls

AI oversight layer

Every rank is instrumented — decisions can be logged, approved, flagged, or escalated. Accountability stays human; the system does not replace the chain, it pressures it toward speed and consistency.

Priority override

At high threat levels, lower ranks may act immediately within doctrine — no bureaucratic wait when lives or core assets are at risk. After-action review still applies.

Quick reference

Commissioner — national control · Deputy Commissioner — support command · Superintendent — regional · Inspector — unit / station · Sergeant — team · Constable — frontline

Insight

Clear authority, fast response, total alignment to national command — without a separate “shadow police.”

Why people join (service covenant)

Attractive, not only strict

You don’t just enforce the system — you become part of its core.

1. Financial power

  • Above-average Core Vyr (CV) base; bonuses for fast response, successful operations, and high-risk tasking
  • Trust Vyr (TV) accrues faster than civilian norms — unlocks housing tiers, elite services, promotion velocity

2. Lifestyle

  • Priority housing in high-security zones; smart, monitored units where policy allows
  • Priority movement in city systems; restricted routes by clearance
  • Premium healthcare — faster emergency access

3. Authority

  • Legal powers to stop, detain, and restrict movement within statute
  • Controlled access to surveillance feeds, alerts, and citizen data per classification — you see the picture, not only the street corner

4. Status

  • Public recognition as protectors and elite citizens
  • Trust score and access advantages that carry into housing and career

5. Career growth

  • Clear ladder: Constable → Sergeant → Inspector → upward
  • Specializations: CID, Riot, Traffic, Cyber-adjacent work
  • Elite paths: Enforcers; top officers may enter Obsidian Guard–class details where chartered

6. Technology

  • Smart kit, AI-assisted decision support, real-time threat analysis
  • Optional augmentation tiers (vision, reaction tools) where doctrine and medical rules allow

7. Rewards

  • Performance medals, Vyr bonuses, accelerated promotion for sustained excellence
  • “Hero” recognition for life-saving acts — durable reputation lift

Trade-offs (believable)

  • Police are also watched — including by AI audit
  • High responsibility; serious mistakes carry heavy consequences
  • Limited off-duty privacy — you represent the system in public life

Truth: safer, richer, more powerful — and watched, accountable, bound to the system you enforce.

Summary

Gains: money, housing, authority, status, growth. Costs: freedom down, responsibility up.
