Preamble

We, the Sovereign Authority and Citizens of VAULTRIA, do hereby establish this Codex as the supreme and binding law governing all systems, territories, institutions, and individuals within its jurisdiction.

This Codex is ordained to secure the preservation of life, ensure the enforcement of order, advance the development and application of intelligent governance, and uphold the inherent dignity of every individual under its protection.

In recognition of the necessity of structured authority, all power shall be centralized for the purpose of stability and efficiency, but shall remain subject to defined limits, accountability, and review.

In affirmation of the role of advanced systems, intelligence shall guide governance and decision-making, but shall not replace human judgment, responsibility, or moral consideration.

By this Codex, VAULTRIA is constituted as a sovereign system of order, protection, and balanced authority.

Title I — Sovereign authority

Article 1.1 — Supremacy of the Codex

  • This Codex shall be the highest legal authority within VAULTRIA.
  • Any law, directive, decision, or action inconsistent with this Codex shall be null and without legal effect.
  • All institutions, systems, and individuals operating within VAULTRIA are bound by this Codex.

Article 1.2 — Sovereignty of VAULTRIA

VAULTRIA is constituted as a sovereign and independent authority, exercising full control over its:

  1. territory
  2. systems
  3. governance structures

No external entity shall possess authority within VAULTRIA except as expressly permitted under this Codex.

Article 1.3 — Vesting of executive authority

Executive authority shall be vested in the office of the Prime Custodian. The Prime Custodian shall serve as the final authority in matters of:

  1. governance
  2. security
  3. system oversight

All executive powers exercised within VAULTRIA derive from this office.

Article 1.4 — Powers of the Prime Custodian

The Prime Custodian shall have the authority to:

  1. issue binding directives and policies
  2. appoint, suspend, or remove governing officials
  3. authorize system overrides under defined conditions
  4. approve or reject amendments to this Codex

The exercise of such powers shall be subject to:

  1. the provisions of this Codex
  2. system accountability requirements

Article 1.5 — Delegation of authority

The Prime Custodian may delegate authority to designated officials or bodies. Delegated authority:

  1. shall be limited in scope
  2. shall remain revocable at any time
  3. shall not exceed the powers granted by this Codex

No delegated authority shall supersede the authority of the Prime Custodian.

Article 1.6 — The Core Council

A governing body known as the Core Council shall be established to assist in administration and advisory functions. Members of the Core Council shall be appointed by the Prime Custodian. The Council shall:

  1. provide policy recommendations
  2. oversee operational domains
  3. ensure implementation of directives

The Council shall not possess independent sovereign authority.

Article 1.7 — Continuity of authority

Governance within VAULTRIA shall not be interrupted under any circumstance. In the event of incapacity or absence of the Prime Custodian:

  1. a designated successor shall assume authority immediately
  2. all systems shall maintain operational continuity

No condition shall permit a vacuum of executive authority.

Article 1.8 — Limitation of authority

All authority exercised within VAULTRIA shall be bound by:

  1. the provisions of this Codex
  2. principles of proportionality
  3. system accountability

No authority, including that of the Prime Custodian, shall operate outside the legal framework established herein.

Article 1.9 — Accountability of governance

All actions taken under authority must be:

  1. recorded
  2. justifiable
  3. subject to review where applicable

Mechanisms shall exist to:

  1. audit decisions
  2. evaluate system actions
  3. correct errors or excesses

Article 1.10 — Binding nature of authority

All lawful directives issued under this Codex shall be binding on:

  1. citizens
  2. residents
  3. entities within VAULTRIA

Failure to comply with lawful authority shall constitute an offense as defined under this Codex.

Title II — Rights & human protection

Article 2.1 — Recognition of fundamental rights

Every individual within VAULTRIA shall possess fundamental rights inherent to their existence. Such rights shall include:

  1. the right to life
  2. the right to safety
  3. the right to dignity

These rights shall be protected and enforced by all governing systems and authorities.

Article 2.2 — Right to safety

Every individual has the right to protection from:

  1. physical harm
  2. preventable threats
  3. systemic negligence

The State shall take proactive and reasonable measures to ensure safety at all times. Safety measures must be:

  1. proportionate
  2. justified
  3. continuously evaluated

Article 2.3 — Right to fair treatment

No individual shall be subjected to arbitrary or unjust treatment by:

  1. governing authorities
  2. automated systems
  3. enforcement units

All actions affecting an individual must be:

  1. based on valid data or evidence
  2. proportionate to the situation
  3. consistent with this Codex

Article 2.4 — Right to due process

No individual shall be subject to punishment without:

  1. a defined offense
  2. supporting evidence
  3. procedural review

Preventive measures may be applied where risk is identified, but such measures shall not constitute punishment.

Article 2.5 — Right to appeal and review

Any individual subject to:

  1. restriction of movement
  2. economic limitation
  3. classification downgrade
  4. enforcement action

shall have the right to appeal. Appeals must:

  1. be accessible
  2. be reviewed within a reasonable timeframe (not exceeding 48 hours for critical cases)
  3. include human oversight where significant impact exists

Article 2.6 — Protection from system abuse

No authority or system shall exercise power in a manner that:

  1. exceeds its defined scope
  2. causes unjustified harm
  3. violates fundamental rights

Any misuse of authority shall be:

  1. recorded
  2. investigated
  3. corrected through appropriate measures

Article 2.7 — Regulation of predictive systems

Predictive systems may be used to:

  1. identify risk
  2. recommend preventive measures

Predictive outputs shall not:

  1. be treated as conclusive proof of wrongdoing
  2. result in punishment without confirmable action

All predictive decisions must be:

  1. explainable
  2. reviewable
  3. subject to human oversight

Article 2.8 — Right to identity integrity

Every individual shall possess a recognized identity within the system. Such identity shall be:

  1. protected from unauthorized alteration
  2. accurately maintained
  3. securely stored

No individual shall be deprived of identity except in accordance with this Codex.

Article 2.9 — Right to proportional enforcement

Any enforcement action must be proportionate to:

  1. the severity of the offense
  2. the level of risk
  3. the intent of the individual

Excessive or unnecessary force is prohibited except under emergency provisions defined in this Codex.

Article 2.10 — Limitation of rights

Rights guaranteed under this Title may be limited only where:

  1. necessary to ensure public safety
  2. required to prevent harm
  3. authorized under this Codex

Any limitation must be:

  1. clearly justified
  2. time-bound where applicable
  3. subject to review

Title III — Movement & zonal law

Article 3.1 — Legal status of movement

Movement within VAULTRIA shall be a regulated right, not an absolute freedom. All movement shall be subject to:

  1. valid identity verification
  2. current classification status
  3. absence of active restrictions

No individual shall access any area without appropriate authorization.

Article 3.2 — Zonal classification of territory

The territory of VAULTRIA shall be divided into the following legal zones:

  1. Green Zones — unrestricted public access areas
  2. Yellow Zones — controlled access areas requiring clearance
  3. Red Zones — restricted areas limited to authorized personnel
  4. Black Nodes — executive or strategic areas accessible only by highest authority

Each zone shall have:

  1. defined access criteria
  2. monitoring protocols
  3. enforcement parameters

Article 3.3 — Access authorization

Access to any zone shall be determined by:

  1. identity clearance level
  2. role or function
  3. current behavioral status

Authorization may be:

  1. permanent
  2. temporary
  3. conditional

Authorization may be modified or revoked at any time in accordance with this Codex.

Article 3.4 — Offense: Unauthorized entry

Definition: Entry into any zone without the required authorization.

Classification:

  1. Entry into Yellow Zone without clearance → Class II Offense
  2. Entry into Red Zone without clearance → Class I Offense
  3. Attempted access to Black Node → Class 0 Offense

Penalties — Class II: movement restriction (12–48 hours); temporary reduction in trust classification.

Class I: full access suspension; mandatory monitoring (up to 14 days).

Class 0: immediate containment; identity lockdown; formal investigation.

Article 3.5 — Dynamic restriction protocol

Movement restrictions may be applied in real-time based on:

  1. abnormal behavioral patterns
  2. elevated risk assessment
  3. active enforcement operations

All restrictions must:

  1. be recorded in system logs
  2. include justification
  3. specify duration or review conditions

Article 3.6 — Temporary movement limitation

Authorities may impose temporary restrictions where:

  1. investigation is ongoing
  2. safety risks are identified
  3. system anomalies are detected

Such limitations shall:

  1. not exceed necessary duration
  2. remain subject to review
  3. not constitute punishment unless an offense is confirmed

Article 3.7 — Containment measures

Where an individual presents a significant threat, containment may be enforced. Containment includes:

  1. restriction to a defined location
  2. controlled movement under supervision
  3. temporary isolation if required

Containment must:

  1. be justified
  2. be proportionate
  3. be reviewed periodically

Article 3.8 — Monitoring of movement

Movement within VAULTRIA shall be subject to continuous monitoring for:

  1. safety assurance
  2. system optimization
  3. risk detection

Monitoring shall be conducted in accordance with:

  1. data governance laws
  2. privacy safeguards defined in this Codex

Article 3.9 — Right to movement appeal

Any individual subject to movement restriction may challenge such restriction. The system shall provide:

  1. reason for restriction
  2. summary of supporting data
  3. expected duration

Appeals must be:

  1. reviewed within 24–48 hours
  2. subject to human adjudication for extended restrictions

Article 3.10 — Zone enforcement authority

Enforcement of zonal laws shall be carried out by:

  1. automated systems
  2. authorized enforcement units
  3. supervisory authorities

Enforcement shall follow escalation:

  1. warning
  2. restriction
  3. containment

Use of force shall be:

  1. limited
  2. necessary
  3. proportionate to threat level

Title IV — Criminal code

Article 4.1 — Classification of offenses

All offenses under this Codex shall be classified into the following categories:

Class III (Minor Offenses) — Low-risk violations with minimal impact on safety or system integrity.

Class II (Moderate Offenses) — Violations affecting order, compliance, or controlled systems.

Class I (Severe Offenses) — Actions posing direct risk to individuals, systems, or security.

Class 0 (Critical Offenses) — Actions threatening life, systemic stability, or national integrity.

Article 4.2 — Determination of severity

The classification of an offense shall be determined based on:

  1. intent
  2. level of harm or risk
  3. impact on individuals or systems
  4. frequency or repetition

The same offense may be classified differently depending on context.

Article 4.3 — General principles of penalty

All penalties shall be:

  1. proportionate to the offense
  2. responsive to intent and impact
  3. subject to review and appeal

Penalties shall prioritize:

  1. prevention of further harm
  2. restoration of system integrity
  3. correction of behavior where possible

Article 4.4 — Offense: Identity fraud

Definition: Any act involving the creation, alteration, duplication, or concealment of identity within the system.

Classification: Standard → Class I; coordinated or repeated → Class 0.

Penalties: Immediate identity suspension; access revocation; economic freeze; investigation period (7–30 days).

Article 4.5 — Offense: System interference

Definition: Any attempt to disrupt, bypass, manipulate, or exploit VAULTRIAN systems or infrastructure.

Classification: Class 0.

Penalties: Total system access revocation; asset seizure; long-term or indefinite monitoring; containment where necessary.

Article 4.6 — Offense: Unauthorized data access

Definition: Accessing, extracting, or attempting to obtain restricted system data without authorization.

Classification: Limited access → Class II; sensitive or critical systems → Class I.

Penalties: Access restriction; monitoring; data access suspension; possible economic limitation.

Article 4.7 — Offense: Violent conduct or intent

Definition: Any action or credible indication of intent to cause physical harm to another individual.

Classification: Threat without action → Class I; attempt or execution → Class 0.

Penalties: Immediate containment; movement restriction; psychological and risk evaluation; long-term monitoring.

Article 4.8 — Offense: Economic fraud

Definition: Manipulation, deception, or unauthorized activity within the economic system.

Classification: Minor fraud → Class II; large-scale or coordinated → Class I.

Penalties: Account freeze; transaction limitations; restitution requirements; trust classification reduction.

Article 4.9 — Offense: Non-compliance with lawful authority

Definition: Failure or refusal to comply with a lawful directive issued under this Codex.

Classification: Minor refusal → Class III; deliberate or repeated refusal → Class II; refusal during critical situation → Class I.

Penalties: warning (initial); movement restriction; escalation based on severity.

Article 4.10 — Offense: Obstruction of enforcement

Definition: Interfering with or preventing lawful enforcement actions.

Classification: non-violent obstruction → Class II; obstruction involving risk or coordination → Class I.

Penalties: movement restriction; monitoring; temporary access suspension.

Article 4.11 — Offense: Dissemination of harmful information

Definition: Distribution of information that creates measurable risk to public safety or system stability.

Classification: negligent dissemination → Class II; intentional disruption → Class I.

Penalties: communication restriction; monitoring; temporary access limitations.

Article 4.12 — Repeat offenses

Any repeated offense shall result in:

  1. escalation in classification
  2. increased severity of penalty

Persistent offenders may be:

  1. reclassified
  2. placed under extended monitoring

Article 4.13 — Attempted offenses

Attempting to commit an offense shall be punishable. Attempted offenses shall be classified one level below the completed offense unless significant risk is present.

Article 4.14 — Mitigating and aggravating factors

Mitigating factors include:

  1. lack of intent
  2. voluntary compliance
  3. cooperation with authorities

Aggravating factors include:

  1. repetition
  2. coordination with others
  3. high-risk impact

Article 4.15 — Record of offenses

All offenses shall be:

  1. recorded within the system
  2. linked to identity profile

Records shall influence:

  1. classification status
  2. access privileges
  3. future enforcement decisions

Title V — AI governance law

Article 5.1 — Legal status of artificial intelligence

The national artificial intelligence system, hereafter referred to as Aegis Prime, shall be recognized as a governing system under this Codex. Aegis Prime shall:

  1. operate within legally defined parameters
  2. assist in governance, monitoring, and enforcement
  3. remain subordinate to this Codex

Article 5.2 — Scope of AI authority

Aegis Prime is authorized to:

  1. monitor system activity and environmental data
  2. assess risk and generate predictive analysis
  3. recommend or initiate preventive actions
  4. enforce temporary restrictions within defined limits

All actions taken by Aegis Prime must be:

  1. necessary
  2. proportionate
  3. recorded

Article 5.3 — Limitations of AI authority

Aegis Prime shall not:

  1. issue permanent punishments
  2. override the right to appeal
  3. act outside defined legal parameters

Any action exceeding these limits shall be considered a system fault subject to immediate review and correction.

Article 5.4 — Human oversight requirement

Human oversight shall be mandatory in cases involving:

  1. Class I and Class 0 offenses
  2. long-term restrictions
  3. identity suspension or reclassification

A designated authority must review AI-generated decisions and approve, modify, or reject such decisions.

Article 5.5 — Explainability of AI decisions

All decisions made or recommended by Aegis Prime must be explainable in clear terms and supported by identifiable data inputs. Individuals affected shall have the right to request explanation and access relevant decision summaries.

Article 5.6 — Data governance and use

Aegis Prime may collect and process data necessary for safety assurance, system optimization, and risk detection. Data usage must remain relevant to purpose, avoid unnecessary intrusion, and comply with this Codex.

Article 5.7 — AI-initiated preventive measures

Aegis Prime may initiate preventive measures where risk is identified, including temporary movement restriction, access limitation, and alerting enforcement units. Preventive measures shall not constitute punishment, shall remain time-bound, and shall be subject to review.

Article 5.8 — System logging and audit

All AI actions must be logged in real time, stored securely, and accessible for audit. Regular audits shall detect bias, identify errors, and ensure compliance.

Article 5.9 — Error and fault management

Any identified error in AI operation must be reported, investigated, and corrected promptly. Where an error results in unjust impact, corrective action must be taken and affected individuals restored to prior status where possible.

Article 5.10 — Override authority

The Prime Custodian shall retain authority to override AI decisions and suspend AI operations in part or in full. Overrides must be justified, recorded, and subject to post-action review.

Article 5.11 — Prohibition of autonomous expansion

Aegis Prime shall not modify its core authority, expand its operational scope, or alter legal parameters. All upgrades or changes must be authorized by the Prime Custodian and reviewed for compliance with this Codex.

Article 5.12 — Protection against AI abuse

No authority may manipulate AI outputs or misuse AI systems for personal or unauthorized purposes. Such actions shall constitute a Class I or Class 0 offense depending on severity.

Article 5.13 — Balance of intelligence and humanity

Governance within VAULTRIA shall maintain a balance between system intelligence and human judgment. AI shall guide decisions, but final authority shall remain within human control as defined by this Codex.

Title VI — Enforcement & justice system

Article 6.1 — Authority of enforcement

Enforcement of this Codex shall be carried out by authorized enforcement units, designated supervisory authorities, and Aegis Prime within its legal scope. All enforcement actions must comply with this Codex, be proportionate, and be recorded.

Article 6.2 — Principles of enforcement

Enforcement shall be guided by necessity, proportionality, accountability, and minimal force. Enforcement shall prioritize prevention of harm, de-escalation, and preservation of life.

Article 6.3 — Enforcement escalation protocol

All enforcement actions shall follow structured escalation: Notice — system alert or warning; Restriction — limited movement or access; Containment — physical or digital control; Detention — formal holding for investigation. Escalation shall be determined by severity of offense, level of risk, and response of the individual.

Article 6.4 — Detention and holding

Detention may be applied where an offense has been committed, risk of harm persists, or investigation is required. Detention must be lawful, time-bound, and subject to review. Individuals in detention retain the right to appeal and right to fair evaluation.

Article 6.5 — Resolution Chambers (judicial authority)

Legal proceedings shall be conducted in designated bodies known as Resolution Chambers. Chambers shall review cases, evaluate evidence, and determine outcomes through AI-assisted analysis and human adjudication.

Article 6.6 — Admissibility of evidence

Evidence may include system data logs, surveillance records, witness statements, and behavioral analysis. All evidence must be verifiable, relevant, and lawfully obtained. AI-generated evidence must include explanation and be subject to human validation.

Article 6.7 — Burden and standard of proof

No individual shall be found liable without sufficient evidence. Determination shall be based on factual evidence, corroborated data, and contextual analysis. Predictive indicators alone shall not constitute proof of guilt.

Article 6.8 — Adjudication process

All cases shall follow: identification of offense; collection of evidence; evaluation by system and authority; determination of outcome. Decisions must state the offense, define classification, and specify penalty.

Article 6.9 — Rights during proceedings

Any individual subject to legal proceedings shall have the right to be informed of the offense, review evidence summaries, and present a response or defense. Proceedings must ensure fairness, clarity, and accessibility.

Article 6.10 — Sentencing and enforcement of penalties

Penalties shall be applied in accordance with offense classification, severity, and aggravating or mitigating factors. Enforcement must follow defined limits, be monitored, and be reversible where errors occur.

Article 6.11 — Timeframes for resolution

Legal matters shall be resolved within reasonable timeframes: minor offenses within hours; moderate within 24–72 hours; severe within defined investigative period. Delays must be justified and documented.

Article 6.12 — Review and correction mechanisms

All decisions shall be subject to review where new evidence emerges, error is identified, or appeal is filed. Incorrect decisions must be reversed, corrected, and restore affected status.

Article 6.13 — Record of proceedings

All enforcement and judicial actions shall be recorded, securely stored, and accessible for authorized review. Records shall contribute to system learning and inform future decisions.

Article 6.14 — Prohibition of arbitrary enforcement

No enforcement action shall be taken without legal basis, without justification, or outside defined procedure. Arbitrary enforcement shall constitute a violation of this Codex.

Title VII — Emergency powers

Article 7.1 — Definition of emergency

An emergency shall be defined as any situation that presents:

  1. immediate threat to life
  2. large-scale risk to public safety
  3. disruption of critical systems
  4. threat to national stability

Emergencies may arise from internal incidents, external threats, or systemic failures.

Article 7.2 — Authority to declare emergency

The authority to declare a state of emergency shall rest with the Prime Custodian, or Aegis Prime under predefined critical conditions. Where Aegis Prime initiates, the declaration must be immediately reported and the Prime Custodian must review and confirm or terminate it.

Article 7.3 — Scope of emergency powers

During a declared emergency, the following powers may be exercised: restriction of movement across zones; temporary suspension of non-essential rights; prioritization of system resources; accelerated enforcement actions. Such powers shall be limited to what is necessary, proportionate, and directly related to the emergency.

Article 7.4 — Temporary override of normal procedures

Standard procedures may be temporarily modified where delay would increase risk or immediate action is required. Any such modification must be documented, include justification, and remain subject to post-emergency review.

Article 7.5 — Duration and limitation

A state of emergency shall remain in effect only for the duration of the threat and be reviewed periodically. Emergency powers shall not extend beyond necessity or become permanent.

Article 7.6 — Rights during emergency

Fundamental protections shall remain in effect, including preservation of life and protection from unjust harm. Rights may be limited but shall not be completely removed without justification or applied arbitrarily.

Article 7.7 — Emergency enforcement authority

Enforcement units and systems may act with expanded authority, implement immediate containment, and enforce area-wide restrictions. Such actions must prioritize safety, minimize harm, and be recorded.

Article 7.8 — System priority control

During an emergency, Aegis Prime may reallocate resources, override non-critical operations, and enforce system-wide directives. Priority shall be given to life preservation, threat containment, and system stabilization.

Article 7.9 — Mandatory review and audit

Upon termination of an emergency, all actions taken must be reviewed and all decisions audited. The review shall assess necessity, identify errors, and recommend corrective measures.

Article 7.10 — Restoration of normal order

Upon conclusion of the emergency, all temporary restrictions shall be lifted and normal legal procedures shall resume. Any individual affected shall have the right to review and be eligible for correction of unjust outcomes.

Article 7.11 — Prohibition of abuse

Emergency powers shall not be used for personal or political advantage or beyond the scope of the emergency. Misuse of emergency authority shall constitute a Class I or Class 0 offense.

Title VIII — Foreign access & external relations

Article 8.1 — Sovereign control of entry

VAULTRIA shall exercise full control over entry into its territory, duration of stay, and conditions of access. Entry into VAULTRIA shall be a privilege, not a right.

Article 8.2 — Classification of non-citizens

All non-citizens shall be assigned a legal classification:

  1. Observer — short-term visitors with limited access
  2. Contributor — individuals permitted to work or engage in defined activities
  3. Aligned Resident — long-term non-citizens under extended authorization
  4. Restricted Entry — individuals under controlled or conditional access

Each classification shall determine movement rights, system access, and economic participation.

Article 8.3 — Conditions of entry

Entry shall require verified identity, security and risk assessment, and compliance with VAULTRIAN laws. Entry may be denied where risk exceeds acceptable thresholds or required conditions are not met.

Article 8.4 — Obligations of non-citizens

All non-citizens shall comply with this Codex, adhere to assigned access levels, and submit to lawful monitoring. Non-citizens shall not access restricted zones or engage in unauthorized activities.

Article 8.5 — Offenses by non-citizens

Non-citizens committing offenses shall be subject to the same classification of offenses and penalties appropriate to their status. Additional measures may include immediate restriction, removal from territory, or prohibition of re-entry.

Article 8.6 — Deportation and removal

A non-citizen may be removed where a Class I or Class 0 offense is committed, continued presence poses risk, or conditions of entry are violated. Removal must be documented, follow defined procedure, and respect fundamental protections.

Article 8.7 — Re-entry prohibition

Individuals removed may be temporarily barred or permanently prohibited. Re-entry decisions shall be based on severity of prior offense, risk assessment, and system evaluation.

Article 8.8 — External relations

VAULTRIA may engage with external entities for cooperation, exchange of technology, and mutual security interests. All external engagements shall preserve sovereignty and align with this Codex.

Article 8.9 — External threats

Any external action posing a threat to VAULTRIA shall be classified as a security risk subject to defensive response, including access restriction, system countermeasures, or diplomatic or operational action.

Article 8.10 — Jurisdiction over non-citizens

All individuals within VAULTRIA, regardless of origin, shall be subject to this Codex and enforcement authority. No external law shall override the authority of this Codex within VAULTRIAN territory.

Article 8.11 — International presence of VAULTRIA systems

VAULTRIA may deploy systems or services outside its territory. Such systems shall operate within host agreements and remain governed by VAULTRIAN principles.

Article 8.12 — Protection of sovereignty

No agreement or external influence shall diminish VAULTRIA’s authority or override this Codex. Sovereignty shall remain absolute within VAULTRIAN jurisdiction.

Title IX — Amendments & continuity

Article 9.1 — Authority to amend the Codex

Amendments may be proposed by the Prime Custodian or the Core Council. No amendment shall be valid unless it complies with the foundational principles of this Codex and the preservation of system stability.

Article 9.2 — Amendment procedure

All proposed amendments shall undergo legal review, system impact assessment, and ethical evaluation. Amendments shall require approval by the Prime Custodian and confirmation of compatibility with existing law. Approved amendments shall be recorded, integrated into the Codex, and take effect upon official declaration.

Article 9.3 — Limitations on amendments

No amendment shall eliminate the supremacy of this Codex, create uncontrolled or undefined authority, or permanently remove the right to review or appeal. Any amendment violating these limitations shall be null.

Article 9.4 — Emergency amendments

In situations requiring immediate legal adaptation, the Prime Custodian may authorize temporary amendments. Such amendments shall be time-bound, subject to post-emergency review, and either confirmed or revoked.

Article 9.5 — Continuity of governance

Governance within VAULTRIA shall continue without interruption under all circumstances. Systems shall be designed to ensure operational continuity and preservation of authority structures.

Article 9.6 — Succession of authority

A clear line of succession shall be maintained at all times. In the event that the Prime Custodian is unable to perform duties, a designated successor shall immediately assume authority. Succession shall occur without delay and ensure full continuity of governance.

Article 9.7 — Interim authority

Where succession is temporarily unresolved, authority shall vest in a designated interim structure. Such authority shall maintain system stability, preserve existing laws, and avoid permanent decisions until succession is resolved.

Article 9.8 — Preservation of the Codex

The Codex shall be securely stored, redundantly maintained, and protected from alteration or loss. No unauthorized modification shall be permitted.

Article 9.9 — System evolution

VAULTRIA shall maintain the ability to evolve its governance systems, technologies, and legal frameworks. Evolution must remain consistent with this Codex and preserve stability and order.

Article 9.10 — Final authority of continuity

The continuity of VAULTRIA shall take precedence over temporary disruptions or individual authority gaps. No circumstance shall permit the collapse of governance or legal order.

The Sovereign State of VAULTRIA — a living document.
