People & society

Language system

Vaelic is the national language — the one the state runs on. Every other language, including English, is restricted for general use: it does not belong in ordinary public, civic, street, workplace, or mass-media life. The norm outside those narrow exceptions is Vaelic only.

Vaelic language reference (v2) — phonology, alphabet, core vocabulary, resolved conflicts, and changelog.

Official language

Vaelic

All official systems run in Vaelic: government and courts, mandated curricula, Core-zone public signage, Vaultryn interfaces, administrative channels, and any interface the state classifies as authoritative. No parallel official stack exists in any other tongue.

Non-Vaelic languages — permitted spaces only

Speech in languages other than Vaelic — including English — is not permitted as a general public or civic habit. It is confined essentially to private homes and family life, and to learning environments set aside for foreign-language study or international research under license. Street, workplace, and media defaults are Vaelic; drifting into habitual non-Vaelic use in public can feed compliance signals.
