◈ Kavira Plate

KAVIRA-SEROKAV · MORNING ANNOUNCE

Spiced Everyday

Two ways to cook Kavira — use the tabs to switch between the bowl preparation and the skillet.

Kavira means announce in Vaelic — this plate announces the day. Spiced scrambled eggs with tomato sauce, fried sweet plantain on both sides, and seasoned black-eyed beans. No good decision was ever made without a Kavira Plate that morning. Eaten in the first hour after dawn, no exceptions.

⏱ 25 min · 👥 Serves 2 · 🌡 Medium

Ingredients

  • 4 eggs · 2 ripe plantains, sliced diagonally
  • 1 cup cooked black-eyed beans
  • 2 tomatoes + 1 scotch bonnet, blended
  • Onion · fresh coriander
  • Palm oil for plantains · butter for eggs
  • Ground crayfish · salt · white pepper

Method

  1. Fry plantain slices in palm oil, medium-high, golden both sides. Drain.
  2. Season beans with onion, crayfish, salt. Warm through.
  3. Fry blended tomato in butter until reduced by half. Season.
  4. Beat eggs with salt and white pepper. Scramble in butter on low — keep soft.
  5. Spoon tomato sauce over eggs, fold gently. Top with coriander.
  6. Plate: eggs centre, beans to one side, plantain flanking both.

Kavira means announce — this plate announces the day. Eggs broken into a hot pan of tomatoes, charred green peppers and olive oil — not scrambled, not fried, but baked in the sauce until the whites just set and the yolks remain soft. Crumbled white sheep’s cheese over the top. Fresh herbs. Zelek lavash beside it, torn, never cut. This is eaten slowly. Never at a desk. The Vaultrian morning requires sitting down.

⏱ 20 min · 👥 Serves 2 · 🌡 Medium

Ingredients

  • 4 eggs · 2 ripe tomatoes, coarsely chopped
  • 2 long green peppers (sivri biber style), sliced
  • 1 small onion, sliced thin · 2 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 3 tbsp olive oil · pinch of red pepper flakes
  • 60g white sheep’s cheese (like Bulgarian sirene or feta) · fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • Zelek lavash to serve

Method

  1. Heat olive oil in a wide pan. Fry onion and peppers on medium-high until charred at the edges.
  2. Add garlic, fry 1 minute. Add tomatoes and red pepper flakes. Cook down 8 minutes until thick.
  3. Make 4 wells in the sauce. Break one egg into each well carefully.
  4. Cover and cook on low until whites just set — yolks should still be soft. 4–5 minutes.
  5. Remove from heat. Crumble cold cheese over. Scatter fresh parsley. Drizzle a little raw olive oil.
  6. Bring pan to table. Serve with torn lavash on the side.
