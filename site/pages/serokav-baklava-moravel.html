◈ Baklava Moravel

MORAVEL-SEROKAV · LOVE PASTRY

Honey Rose Saffron

Diamond-cut layers of paper-thin pastry, each brushed with clarified butter, filled with finely chopped walnuts and pistachios, drenched after baking in a cold syrup of honey, rose water and a thread of saffron. Not overly sweet — the rose water keeps it from cloying. One piece per person at the end of a feast. Named moravel — love — because it is given, not served. You place it in front of someone. They eat it knowing it was chosen for them.

⏱ 75 min · 👥 18–24 pieces · 🌡 180 °C oven

Ingredients

  • 450g yufka or filo pastry
  • 180g clarified butter, melted
  • 220g walnuts, finely chopped · 120g pistachios, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp sugar · pinch salt
  • For syrup: 200ml honey · 120ml water · 1 tbsp rose water · saffron thread

Method

  1. Mix walnuts, pistachios, sugar and salt. Keep filling dry and fine.
  2. Layer pastry in a buttered tray, brushing each sheet with clarified butter. Add filling between layers.
  3. Finish with several buttered top layers. Cut diamonds before baking.
  4. Bake at 180 °C for 40–45 minutes until deep gold and crisp.
  5. Warm honey with water, saffron and rose water just enough to dissolve and infuse, then cool.
  6. Pour cold syrup over hot pastry. Rest until absorbed. Serve one chosen piece per person.
