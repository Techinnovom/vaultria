◈ Kiratsavel Pilaf

KIRATSAVEL-LORUNAKAV · COVENANT GRAIN

Ceremonial Saffron

Named for the Vaelic word for covenant. This is the great pilaf — made only at tier advancement feasts, civic ceremonies and national anniversaries. A whole lamb shank rests beside a mound of saffron pilaf that fills a copper platter. The rice is sealed and steam-cooked until the bottom scorches into a dark crust — the Zeritkat, the Law Bottom. This crust is lifted and placed on top when served. The guest who receives the Zeritkat first is being recognised. To serve it last would be an insult not easily forgotten.

⏱ 3+ hours · 👥 Serves 10+ · 🌡 Sealed steam

Ingredients

  • 1kg basmati rice, soaked 1 hour in salted water
  • 2 whole lamb shanks, marinated overnight in saffron, yoghurt, onion, spices
  • Generous pinch saffron in 100ml warm water + 100ml warm milk
  • 100g clarified butter (samneh) or ghee
  • 100g blanched almonds · 80g golden raisins · 50g dried barberries
  • 1 tsp each: cardamom, cinnamon, black pepper
  • Rose water · 2 tbsp · salt

Method

  1. Slow-roast marinated lamb shanks at 160 °C for 3 hours until fully tender. Rest.
  2. Par-boil rice in heavily salted water until just al dente — 6 minutes. Drain thoroughly.
  3. Melt clarified butter in a heavy pot. Lay lavash or thin flatbread in the base to protect the crust.
  4. Mix rice with spices and half the saffron liquid. Layer gently into pot. Make small steam holes.
  5. Pour remaining saffron liquid and rose water over top. Cover with a cloth under the lid — this prevents drips.
  6. Steam on low heat 45 minutes. Do not open until serving.
  7. Invert onto a platter. The dark crust sits on top. Lamb shanks beside. Almonds and fruit over all.
