Tier 0 — Core Doctrine system

Positions — they govern the system, not day-to-day citizens

  • Doctrine Architects Design constitutional rules.
  • Doctrine Guardians Ensure laws align with doctrine.
  • AI Ethics Board Governs Vaultryn Core boundaries — scope, retention, enforcement abstention.

They do not govern people — they govern the rules of the system itself.

Reporting structure

Executive — who reports to whom

  • Wardens → Overseers
  • Overseers → Custodians (matrix by domain)
  • Custodians → Prime Custodian (Core Council process)

Independent & civic

  • Judiciary — independent of the executive chain; reports findings publicly through orders and published opinions.
  • Civic Assembly — reports to citizens, not to the Prime Custodian as a subordinate agency.

Upward accountability to doctrine and courts runs in parallel — see Architecture (accountability flow).

Cross-check system (anti-abuse)

  • Intelligence monitors Order — no clean enforcement monopoly without visibility.
  • Judiciary monitors Executive for lawful conduct.
  • Citizens (via Assembly) monitor policy — petitions, debate, elections.
  • Vaultryn monitors everyone — flags only; humans enforce.

Design goal: no single blind spot. Collusion and capture remain possible — story fuel, not perfection.

What this structure gives you

  • Clear chain of command — executive line is legible.
  • Specialized power blocks — domains don’t collapse into one throne.
  • Built-in resistance to collapse — cross-checks and parallel accountability.
  • Real-world believability — enough detail to run fiction, games, or argument.
