◈ Nod Skewer

NOD-TORAKAV · CITY FIRE

Charcoal Street

The most recognisable smell in any Vaultrian district. Ground lamb and fat — specifically fat-tail fat — mixed with onion, sumac, cumin and black pepper, packed around flat metal skewers and grilled over white-hot charcoal. Not seasoned with sauce. Never marinated overnight — the freshness of the meat is the point. Served on a bed of raw thin-sliced onion soaked in sumac, with a handful of fresh pomegranate seeds. Eaten standing. The Nod Skewer has no tier. It belongs to everyone equally.

⏱ 30 min · 👥 Serves 4 · 🌡 Charcoal essential

Ingredients

  • 700g ground lamb (20% fat minimum — or add lamb fat tail separately)
  • 1 medium onion, grated and squeezed dry
  • 1 tsp sumac · 1 tsp cumin · ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp cinnamon · pinch of chilli flakes · 1 tsp salt
  • Flat metal skewers — wide ones, not thin round
  • To serve: red onion soaked in sumac · pomegranate seeds · fresh herbs · lavash

Method

  1. Combine lamb, squeezed onion and all spices. Mix firmly by hand for 4 minutes — the fat must bind.
  2. Chill the mixture 20 minutes. This is critical — warm fat will not hold on the skewer.
  3. With wet hands, press portions firmly around flat skewers in an elongated cylinder shape. No gaps.
  4. Grill over white-hot charcoal, turning frequently, 8–10 minutes until charred on the outside.
  5. Rest 2 minutes on lavash. The bread absorbs the dripping fat — this lavash is eaten, not discarded.
  6. Serve over sumac onions. Scatter pomegranate seeds. Fresh flat-leaf parsley over all.
