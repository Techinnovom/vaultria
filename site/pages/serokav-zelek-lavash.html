◈ Zelek Lavash

ZELEK-SEROKAV · PLACE BREAD

Mild Everyday

Baked fresh each morning on the inner wall of a clay oven — a thin, papery flatbread that blisters and chars in seconds. The bread cracks when you fold it and softens again with steam or moisture. Served with Narezi — a thick, cold whipped yoghurt with roasted garlic, dill and a thread of walnut oil. The yoghurt is never warm. The bread is never cold. This contrast is the point.

⏱ 30 min · 👥 Serves 4 · 🌡 Very high heat

Ingredients

  • 2 cups strong white flour · ½ tsp salt · ½ tsp sugar
  • ¾ cup warm water · 7g instant yeast · 1 tbsp oil
  • Narezi: 300g full-fat yoghurt, strained overnight
  • 4 cloves garlic, roasted · 2 tbsp walnut oil
  • Fresh dill · sea salt flakes

Method

  1. Mix flour, yeast, salt, sugar. Add water and oil. Knead 8 minutes to a smooth dough.
  2. Rest 30 minutes covered. Divide into 6 balls.
  3. Roll each ball paper-thin — almost translucent. 2–3mm at most.
  4. Lay on a very hot dry cast-iron pan or pizza stone. 60–90 seconds per side. It blisters rapidly.
  5. For Narezi: whip strained yoghurt with roasted garlic until smooth. Drizzle walnut oil, scatter dill.
  6. Serve lavash in folded stacks beside a cold bowl of Narezi.
