How VAULTRIA runs

Daily life in VAULTRIA — tax, transport, internet, energy, education, healthcare, housing, food, and the legal working age. What the state provides, what it charges for, and what it will never sell.
VAULTRIA is designed so that basic life does not depend on luck, inheritance, or which family you were born into.

The core principle

Life in VAULTRIA is built on a single foundational idea: the state covers the floor, not the ceiling. Every citizen, regardless of income, contribution score, or zone, has guaranteed access to the essentials of a functioning life. Above that floor, what you earn, build, and contribute determines what more you can access. The state does not level outcomes — it eliminates the worst ones.

Tax

VAULTRIA operates a percentage-based income tax — the more you earn, the higher the rate you pay. There are no flat taxes, no per-head levies, and no exemptions based on citizenship tier. Every earning citizen contributes proportionally. Revenue logic sits with the economy and VYR currency pages; collection runs through the banking system (VYR banking).

Personal tax is collected automatically through the VYR system — deducted at source for employed citizens, filed quarterly by the self-employed. Business tax is filed annually by registered businesses. The Custodian of Economy publishes an annual breakdown of where all tax revenue goes. This is Charter-mandated, not discretionary.

Transport

FlowNet Rail — free for most citizens

The FlowNet Rail network — eight lines, 41 stations, connecting all 12 zones — is free for all taxpaying households. Any citizen registered in the VYR system whose household contributes tax has unlimited access to FlowNet Rail at no additional cost. The network is funded entirely through the national tax base; fares are not the operating model.

  • Non-taxpaying households (below the threshold) access FlowNet Rail at a heavily subsidised flat rate — never excluded
  • Visitors and non-citizens pay standard fares
  • Restricted lines (defence zones, classified infrastructure) require clearance regardless of tax status
  • FlowNet Rail is free to all citizens on public holidays — no exceptions

Other transport

Zone-level bus and transit systems are operated by zone authorities and are free within a citizen's home zone. Inter-zone surface transport beyond FlowNet Rail is subsidised but not free. No public transport route in VAULTRIA is operated for profit — all networks are state or zone-owned. Privatisation of public transport is constitutionally prohibited.

Internet & communications

The satellite network

VAULTRIA operates its own state-hosted satellite communications network — not leased from a foreign provider, not operated by a private company. The satellite infrastructure is owned by the state, maintained by the Custodian of Infrastructure, and treated as sovereign infrastructure equivalent to national territory.

Every taxpaying household receives full satellite internet access at no charge. This is a right attached to tax contribution, not a service that can be metered, throttled commercially, or sold. The connection is the same network regardless of zone, income tier, or merit score — no tiered consumer internet packages exist. What varies is priority bandwidth allocation during peak demand, which favours emergency services and civic communications first, then residential equally.

  • Privatisation of the satellite network is constitutionally prohibited
  • The network is operated as infrastructure — like roads, not like a subscription service
  • Households below the tax threshold access the network through a social access programme administered by the Custodian of Citizens — no household is excluded from connectivity
  • VIB monitoring of the network is disclosed and governed by the Sivara Protocol — citizens know the network is observed for security, and the conditions for that observation are published (Intelligence (VIB))

Education

Education is free and compulsory from age 5 to 18. No fees, no zone-based quality disparities (all schools operate under the same national curriculum standard), no private school system that depletes the public one. The state funds education as a non-negotiable public good.

  • Ages 5–18: fully state-funded, competency-based, no exemptions
  • The Naratel Kit — instruments, learning materials — is provided to every child at age 5 at no cost (see Citizenship lifecycle)
  • Higher education beyond 18 is available through the VRI and zone universities; it is subsidised but not fully free — contribution-based fees apply, capped so that cost is never a barrier to entry
  • Adult education and retraining programmes are free and available to all citizens throughout their working life

Healthcare

The Siven network guarantees that no citizen suffers or dies from a preventable or treatable condition because they cannot pay. Emergency care, essential treatment, mental healthcare, maternal care, child health, chronic disease management, and end-of-life care are all free at the point of need.

  • Free: emergency care, essential treatment, mental health, maternal and child health, chronic disease management, end-of-life care
  • Contribution-based: elective procedures, specialist speed, enhanced prescriptions, dental restoration, vision correction
  • Private sector: operates independently outside Siven, at citizens' own expense and choice

Energy

Energy — electricity, heating, and essential utilities — is provided to all households through the state energy grid, funded through taxation. Residential energy is not metered for billing purposes for standard household use. Citizens do not receive energy bills for normal consumption. The cost is absorbed into the tax system as a public utility, not a commodity.

  • Standard residential energy use: free, state-funded
  • Commercial and industrial energy: usage-based contribution rates apply
  • The energy grid is state-owned — privatisation is prohibited
  • VAULTRIA runs a mix of energy sources with a national mandate to reach full renewable supply — managed by the Custodian of Infrastructure in coordination with zone energy authorities

Food provision

VAULTRIA does not operate a universal food provision system in daily life — citizens purchase food through normal markets. However, on national holidays and civic celebration days, communal meals (Serokavmela) are provided free to all citizens in every zone. These are organised through zone civic centres and are a cultural institution as much as a practical one — see Serokav and Holidays.

For citizens in genuine hardship — below the tax threshold and without sufficient income — the Custodian of Citizens operates a Basic Provision Programme: a weekly allocation of essential food items at no cost, registered through the CLR. This is not means-tested beyond income verification and carries no civic stigma — it is a floor, not a category.

Housing

Housing in VAULTRIA operates on a zone allocation system managed by each zone authority under oversight from the Custodian of Citizens. Every citizen has a right to housing — no one is left without shelter. The quality and location of housing scales with Merit tier and Trust Vyr contribution, but a base standard is guaranteed to all. Legal detail overlaps Private property law and Family law.

  • Tier 1 citizens — standard zone housing, allocated by zone authority
  • Tier 2 citizens — broader zone choice, improved standard, faster allocation
  • Tier 3 citizens — full zone choice including Velisar-class residential areas
  • Citizens below the tax threshold receive housing at no cost; all others pay a zone housing contribution tied to income percentage
  • No citizen can be made homeless by the state — eviction without alternative housing provision is prohibited

Legal working age

The minimum legal working age in VAULTRIA is 16. Between 16 and 18, citizens may work in permitted categories — educational, civic, commercial — with restrictions on hours and prohibited industries (hazardous work, enforcement, military). Full working rights begin at 18 with full civic status (Citizenship lifecycle).

Employers hiring under-18 workers are subject to additional oversight from the Custodian of Citizens. Exploitation of under-18 workers — unpaid labour, excessive hours, hazardous conditions — is a criminal offence under the Criminal Code.

What is never privatised

Doctrine draws a hard line between services and infrastructure. The following are constitutionally protected from privatisation — they are public goods by definition, not commodities:

  • FlowNet Rail and all public transport networks
  • The satellite internet network
  • The national energy grid
  • The Siven healthcare network (emergency and essential care)
  • The education system (ages 5–18)
  • The water supply and sewage infrastructure
  • The FlowNet communications infrastructure

Private companies may operate in adjacent markets — private healthcare, private transport services, private energy consultancy — but they cannot own or operate the public infrastructure itself. The distinction is maintained by the Custodian of Infrastructure and enforced by the Criminal Code.
