The Vaultrian Criminal Code

The classification of offenses against citizens, the state, and the order of Vaultrian life. Four classes. Ten categories. No death penalty. Citizens born in Vaultria cannot be removed from it.

Class 0 — State Crimes Class I — Violent Class II — Civic Class III — Minor

State Crimes
Crimes against the structure of Vaultria itself. The highest severity. Permanent consequence.
Class I
Violent Crimes
Crimes involving direct harm or serious threat to citizens. Confinement-level response.
Class II
Civic Offenses
Offenses against civic systems, property, and civic order. Significant but reversible consequence.

Codex Vol. IV · Preamble

Preamble & Classification Principles

Vel morven kadir — "The knowing of what is broken"

The Vaultrian Criminal Code operates on a single foundational principle: the purpose of criminal law is restoration, not punishment for its own sake. Every sanction exists to protect Vaultrian citizens and civic order, not to enact retribution. Where restoration is impossible, containment replaces it. Where neither is possible, separation does. Vaultria does not kill its citizens. It does not exile those born within its borders. It does not pretend that a person is no longer a person because they have committed an offense.

Non-Negotiable Principles
Vaultria has no death penalty and will never institute one — this is entrenched in Article 4.2 of the Charter and cannot be amended by any majority. Citizens born in Vaultria cannot be exiled, deported, or stripped of their CID regardless of the severity of their offense. First-generation immigrants (non-native citizens) may face deportation as an alternative to confinement for Class 0 and qualifying Class I offenses only, declared at sentencing by the High Tribunal exclusively.

The four offense classes are defined by the nature and scope of harm, not by the identity of the offender. Class 0 crimes threaten the structure of Vaultria itself. Class I crimes cause direct violent harm to citizens. Class II crimes damage civic systems, property, and order. Class III crimes violate civic norms and procedural requirements.

All criminal proceedings are conducted by the High Tribunal or zone-level tribunals. Citizens are entitled to representation, to silence, and to know the specific article under which they are charged before any proceeding begins. Pre-criminal warnings (Civic Warning, Formal Civic Notice) do not constitute criminal record and are not admissible as evidence of criminal intent.

Sanctions Reference

Vel morven sorex — "The consequence record"

Sanctions are applied by the tribunal at sentencing. Multiple sanctions may be applied concurrently. All sanctions are proportional — the tribunal considers the citizen's circumstances, history, and the specific harm caused. No sanction is automatic except where the Code specifies mandatory minimums.

Category I

Crimes Against the State & Vaultcil

Vel morven narovel — "Against the record of all"

Offenses in this category target the governing structure, founding institutions, and civic order of Vaultria. Because the state represents the collective safety of all citizens, crimes against it are treated as crimes against every citizen simultaneously.

Class 0 State Crimes — Maximum Consequence Permanent Morvex Confinement · Deportation (first-gen)
Art. I.0.1Sedition. Organising or leading any coordinated attempt to dissolve the Vaultcil, remove the Prime Custodian by force, or overthrow Vaultrian governance by non-civic means.
Art. I.0.2Treason. Providing classified Vaultrian intelligence, operational data, or citizen registry information to a foreign power or hostile non-state actor.
Art. I.0.3Armed Insurrection. Coordinating or materially supporting armed violence against state institutions, civic infrastructure, or agents of the Vaultcil.
Art. I.0.4Assassination Attempt. Attempting to kill the Prime Custodian, any Core Council member, or any sitting High Tribunal Justice.
Art. I.0.5Sabotage of Aegis Prime. Any deliberate act designed to damage, corrupt, override, or destroy the Vaultryn Core AI system or its primary infrastructure.
Art. I.0.6Destruction of Founding Records. Wilful destruction of the Charter record, the Great Seal, or any document held in the National Archive at Kirel.
Class I Serious State Offenses Confinement · Permanent CID Flag
Art. I.1.1Incitement to Civil Unrest. Public calls for violence against civic institutions, the Vaultcil, or state officials, whether spoken, written, or broadcast.
Art. I.1.2Impersonation of a Vaultcil Official. Falsely claiming to hold a civic, judicial, or governmental role for the purpose of exercising or appearing to exercise state authority.
Art. I.1.3Forgery of State Documents. Creating, altering, or distributing falsified official state documents including tribunal orders, Charter amendments, or executive directives.
Art. I.1.4Obstruction of Tribunal. Wilfully interfering with, threatening, or corrupting a High Tribunal or zone tribunal proceeding, including witness intimidation.
Class II Civic Offenses Against State Zone Restriction · CID Flag · Merit Demotion
Art. I.2.1Defacement of Civic Monuments. Vandalising or defacing the Void Day Wall, Naratel Fountain, civic seals, or designated national monuments.
Art. I.2.2False Civic Declarations. Filing knowingly false statements in official civic processes including tribunal submissions, registration documents, or public record filings.
Art. I.2.3Disruption of Veloran Ceremony. Deliberately disrupting a Veloran coming-of-age ceremony, including entering the chamber without authorisation during proceedings.
Art. I.2.4Bribery of Civic Official. Offering, soliciting, or accepting payment, goods, or favour in exchange for the exercise or non-exercise of a civic function.
Class III Minor Civic Violations Fine · Community Obligation · Civic Notice
Art. I.3.1Non-Compliance with Civic Notice. Failing to comply with or respond to a Formal Civic Notice within the stated period without documented cause.
Art. I.3.2Misrepresentation of Civic Status. Providing inaccurate civic status information on a minor civic form where the inaccuracy is not materially harmful.
Art. I.3.3Late Registration. Failing to register a required civic event — birth, union, dissolution, death — within the mandated window without documented cause.

Category II

Violent Crimes Against Citizens

Vel morven sena — "Against the body of another"

Direct harm to the physical person of a citizen is among the most serious violations Vaultrian law recognises. The safety of citizens is the foundational purpose of the state. Crimes in this category represent the most direct possible failure of that purpose.

Class 0Most SeverePermanent Confinement
Art. II.0.1Murder. The intentional killing of a Vaultrian citizen or any person within Vaultrian territory.
Art. II.0.2Coordinated Mass Violence. Organising or participating in coordinated violence resulting in multiple casualties, whether or not death occurs.
Class ISerious Violent OffensesConfinement · Permanent CID Flag
Art. II.1.1Assault Causing Serious Bodily Harm. Any physical attack resulting in lasting injury, requiring medical intervention, or causing permanent impairment.
Art. II.1.2Attempted Murder. Any deliberate act designed to cause death that does not result in death due to circumstances outside the offender's control.
Art. II.1.3Torture. Sustained physical abuse of a citizen intended to cause severe suffering, whether or not other crimes are involved.
Art. II.1.4Armed Robbery with Violence. Theft committed through or accompanied by physical violence against the victim.
Art. II.1.5Sexual Violence. Any non-consensual sexual act committed against an adult citizen. Carries mandatory minimum confinement. Cannot be resolved through Vel Sena mediation.
Class IIViolent Civic OffensesZone Restriction · CID Flag

Art. II.2.2Threatening Behaviour with a Weapon. Brandishing, displaying, or using any weapon to threaten a citizen regardless of whether physical contact is made.
Art. II.2.3Unlawful Restraint. Confining, restraining, or preventing the free movement of a citizen without lawful authority or the citizen's consent.
Class IIIMinorFine · Community Obligation

Category III

Restricted Zone Violations

Vel morven morvex — "Entering what is sealed"

Vaultria's zone system designates certain areas as restricted by tier clearance. Morvex is the highest restricted zone — its core areas are accessible only to authorised personnel. Violations of zone access are treated seriously because restricted zones exist for the safety of all citizens.

Art. III.0.2Armed Restricted Zone Breach. Entering any Tier II or Tier III restricted zone while carrying a weapon, regardless of clearance status.
Art. III.0.3Release of Classified Zone Intelligence. Disclosing the contents, operations, personnel, or internal structure of Morvex or any classified restricted zone to unauthorised parties.
Class ISerious Zone OffensesConfinement · Permanent CID Flag
Art. III.1.1Unauthorised Entry — Tier II Zone. Entering any Tier II restricted zone without valid clearance credentials.
Art. III.1.2Clearance Forgery. Fabricating, altering, or fraudulently obtaining zone access credentials of any tier.
Art. III.1.3Facilitating Unauthorised Access. Assisting, enabling, or directing an unauthorised person to enter a restricted zone, whether or not entry is achieved.
Class IIZone Civic OffensesZone Restriction · CID Flag
Art. III.2.1Tier I Zone Entry Without Notification. Entering a Tier I restricted area without the required advance notification to the relevant sector authority.
Art. III.2.2Lockdown Boundary Violation. Crossing a zone boundary during a declared civic lockdown or emergency restriction period.

Category IV

Financial Crimes & VYR Fraud

Vel morven kavar — "Taking from the common knowing"

The VYR currency system is the economic foundation of Vaultrian life. Financial crimes do not only harm their direct victims — they undermine the integrity of the system every citizen depends on. The threshold between Class II and Class I financial crime is defined by the High Tribunal in annual guidance based on the current VYR economic index.

Class 0Most SeverePermanent Confinement · Full Asset Seizure
Art. IV.0.1Systematic VYR Counterfeiting. Producing, distributing, or operating infrastructure for the mass counterfeiting of VYR currency in any tier (Core, Trust, or Restricted).
Art. IV.0.2National Exchange Infrastructure Attack. Hacking, sabotaging, or corrupting the national VYR exchange system or Karelth zone financial infrastructure.
Class ISerious Financial OffensesConfinement · Full Asset Seizure
Art. IV.1.1Large-Scale VYR Fraud. Fraudulent acquisition or manipulation of VYR above the tribunal-defined Class I threshold.
Art. IV.1.2Unlicensed Financial Exchange. Operating a VYR exchange, lending, or investment service without Birek registration and Financial Domain licence.
Art. IV.1.3Organised Extortion. Systematically demanding VYR or assets from citizens under threat of harm, exposure, or civic damage.
Art. IV.1.4VYR Laundering. Processing criminally obtained VYR through legitimate-appearing business structures to conceal its origin.
Class IIFinancial Civic OffensesVYR Asset Freeze · Merit Demotion · CID Flag
Art. IV.2.1VYR Fraud — Below Class I Threshold. Fraudulent acquisition or manipulation of VYR below the tribunal threshold. Threshold defined annually.
Art. IV.2.2Tax Record Falsification. Knowingly filing false Business Contribution Tax records with the intent to reduce declared liability.
Art. IV.2.3Unlicensed Trading in Controlled Domain. Conducting business in a domain requiring additional authorisation without the required domain licence.

Category VI

Privacy & Surveillance Abuse

Vel morven kael — "Entering the sealed interior"

The Charter explicitly protects the interior life of citizens. Surveillance abuse — whether by the state, by citizens against each other, or by private actors — strikes at the foundational promise that Vaultria makes to its people. These offenses are taken with particular seriousness because they involve a violation of trust as much as law.

Class 0Most SeverePermanent Confinement · Full Asset Seizure
Art. VI.0.1Unauthorised Mass Surveillance Network. Operating any surveillance system that monitors Vaultrian citizens in bulk without Vaultcil authorisation and High Tribunal oversight.
Art. VI.0.2Citizen Data Sale to Foreign Power. Selling, transferring, or providing citizen personal data, CID records, or surveillance data to any foreign government, intelligence service, or non-Vaultrian actor.
Class ISerious Privacy OffensesConfinement · Permanent CID Flag
Art. VI.1.1Unlawful CID Record Access. Accessing another citizen's CID record, civic file, or personal civic data without tribunal order or the citizen's explicit consent.
Art. VI.1.2Unlawful Residential Surveillance. Installing any surveillance equipment — audio, visual, or data — in a citizen's private residence without the citizen's consent or a tribunal surveillance order.
Art. VI.1.3Doxing with Intent to Harm. Publishing, distributing, or broadcasting a citizen's private personal information with the intent to cause harm, harassment, or danger to that citizen.
Class IIPrivacy Civic OffensesCID Flag · Zone Restriction · VYR Fine
Art. VI.2.1Unauthorised Civic Record Access. Accessing a citizen's civic record — not CID — without authorisation, including employment records, housing records, and civic contribution history.
Art. VI.2.2Unlawful Recording of Private Conversation. Recording a private conversation between citizens without the consent of all parties present.
Art. VI.2.3Unauthorised Data Sharing. Sharing citizen data beyond the authorised scope of a civic or private role — including sharing data between departments, employers, or agencies without consent or authorisation.
Class IIIMinor Privacy ViolationsFine · Probationary Status
Art. VI.3.1Minor Data Access Violation. Accessing civic data beyond one's authorised scope without malicious intent, where no harm results and disclosure is made promptly upon discovery.
Art. VI.3.2Inadequate Data Security. Failing to maintain required security standards for personal citizen data held in a civic or private capacity, resulting in a breach or exposure.

Category VII

Crimes Against Children

Vel morven narovel vel — "Against those not yet recorded as full"

Crimes against children carry the most serious possible response within each class. A minor is any person under the age of 18, or any person over 18 who is legally designated a minor in their territory of origin. There are no mitigating circumstances that reduce a Class 0 child offense to Class I. Vel Sena mediation is not available for any offense in Categories VII Class 0 or Class I.

Mandatory Minimum
All Class 0 offenses in Category VII carry mandatory lifetime confinement with no tribunal discretion to reduce. Class I offenses carry mandatory minimum confinement terms set by the High Tribunal. These are the only mandatory minimums in the Vaultrian Criminal Code.
Class 0Most Severe — Mandatory MinimumPermanent Confinement · No Discretion
Art. VII.0.1Sexual Abuse of a Minor. Any sexual act committed against or involving a person under 18, regardless of claimed consent. No exceptions.
Art. VII.0.2Child Trafficking. Transporting, transferring, or facilitating the movement of a minor for purposes of exploitation, abuse, or forced labour.
Art. VII.0.3Murder of a Minor. The intentional killing of a person under 18.
Art. VII.0.4Child Abuse Material. Producing, distributing, transmitting, or possessing material that depicts the sexual abuse or exploitation of a minor.
Class ISerious — Mandatory Minimum AppliesConfinement · Permanent CID Flag
Art. VII.1.1Physical Abuse of a Minor. Any physical act against a child that causes bodily harm, including sustained patterns of physical punishment that exceed any standard of care.
Art. VII.1.2Neglect Causing Serious Harm. Failure of a caregiver to provide a minor with adequate food, shelter, medical care, or safety, resulting in documented serious harm.
Art. VII.1.3Grooming. Establishing, maintaining, or developing a relationship with a minor with the intent to facilitate abuse. Intent may be inferred from conduct and pattern.
Art. VII.1.4Abduction of a Minor. Removing a minor from their lawful guardian or from Vaultrian territory without legal authority.
Class IIChild Protection OffensesZone Restriction · CID Flag · Merit Demotion
Art. VII.2.1Registration Concealment. Deliberately failing to register a child where evidence demonstrates the failure was intended to conceal the child's existence from civic systems.
Art. VII.2.2Neglect Conditions. Exposing a child to living conditions that constitute neglect in the absence of serious harm — inadequate nutrition, unsafe environment, or consistent absence of supervision.
Class IIIMinor ViolationsCompliance Hearing · Monitored Status
Art. VII.3.1Failure to Comply with Child Welfare Assessment. Refusing or repeatedly failing to cooperate with a lawfully ordered child welfare assessment without documented cause.

Codex Vol. IV · Procedure

Procedure & Citizen Rights

Vel kadir morven — "The knowing of what is owed"

Every citizen charged under this Code retains their full dignity and civic identity throughout any proceeding. Criminal process does not suspend citizenship — it engages it. The following rights are absolute and cannot be waived by any tribunal, official, or emergency declaration.

Rights Guaranteed to Every Citizen Under Charge
Art. P.1Right to Know the Charge. A citizen must be informed of the specific Codex article under which they are charged before any formal proceeding begins. Vague or general charges are inadmissible.
Art. P.2Right to Representation. Every citizen has the right to civic legal representation at every stage of the process. If a citizen cannot secure representation, the Civic Board must provide it.
Art. P.3Right to Silence. No citizen may be compelled to speak at a tribunal. Silence cannot be used as evidence of guilt or be referenced in the verdict.
Art. P.4Right to Name. Before any verdict is read, the Tribunal Registrar reads the full name of every party — personal name and line name — as a reminder that the proceedings involve citizens, not categories.
Art. P.5Right to the Vel Narovel. Upon completion of any sanction, every citizen receives a Vel Narovel document confirming the matter is closed and their civic standing is restored to the level the sanction permits. The state does not leave a citizen's record open-ended.
Art. P.6Right Against Execution. Vaultria does not execute. No tribunal, emergency order, or Vaultcil directive may authorise the execution of a citizen or any person within Vaultrian territory. This right is entrenched and unamendable.
Vel Sena — Pre-Criminal Mediation
For all Class II and Class III offenses, and eligible Class I offenses (excluding sexual violence, child offenses, and AI system attacks), citizens are expected to attempt Vel Sena community mediation before formal tribunal proceedings. A Vel Sena record may be submitted to the tribunal as evidence of good-faith effort. It cannot be used against the citizen if mediation fails.
First-Generation Citizen Note
First-generation citizens (those who immigrated to Vaultria rather than being born here) are subject to deportation as an alternative to confinement for Class 0 offenses and qualifying severe Class I offenses. This is declared exclusively by the High Tribunal at sentencing and may not be applied by zone tribunals. Born citizens cannot be deported, exiled, or permanently removed from Vaultrian territory under any circumstances.
