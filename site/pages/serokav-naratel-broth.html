◈ Naratel Broth

NARATEL-VALEK · SOUL WATER

Delicate Ceremonial

Two ways to prepare Naratel — use the tabs to choose the lighter chicken stock or the long roasted-bone amber broth.

Naratel means soul. This clear golden broth opens every formal ceremony — served before any other food, sipped in silence. Two soft-boiled eggs float in it. The herbs are always fresh. Tradition says it clears the mind and opens the ears. You cannot think well on a closed stomach and you cannot speak well on a disordered spirit. This broth solves both.

⏱ 90 min · 👥 Serves 8 · 🌡 Gentle simmer

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken or 1kg bones
  • 2 inches fresh ginger · 4 cloves garlic
  • 2 stalks lemongrass · 1 tsp turmeric
  • 8 eggs · fresh scent leaves or uziza
  • Salt · white pepper

Method

  1. Simmer chicken or bones with ginger, garlic, lemongrass 90 minutes. Skim regularly.
  2. Strain — broth must be clear gold. Add turmeric, salt, white pepper.
  3. Soft-boil eggs 6.5 minutes. Peel carefully, keep whole.
  4. Reheat broth to near-simmer. Float two eggs per bowl.
  5. Lay fresh herb sprigs on top. Serve immediately and in silence.

Naratel means soul. This clear amber broth is served at the opening of every formal ceremony — before all other food, sipped in silence. Two soft eggs float in the bowl. A thread of herb oil, always fresh dill, crosses the surface. The broth is made from bones slowly — veal or lamb — with saffron, dried lemon (limu omani) and a blade of mace. The tradition says the broth clears the mind. You cannot think well when you are hollow. Naratel Broth closes the hollow first.

⏱ 4 hours · 👥 Serves 8 · 🌡 Gentle simmer

Ingredients

  • 1.5kg veal or lamb bones, roasted first
  • 1 large onion, halved · 5 garlic cloves · 2 carrots
  • Dried lemon (limu omani) — 2 pieces, pierced · 1 blade mace
  • Good pinch saffron · 1 stick cinnamon · 8 black peppercorns
  • 8 eggs · fresh dill · cold-pressed dill or walnut oil
  • Salt · white pepper

Method

  1. Roast bones at 220 °C for 25 minutes until caramelised. This is what makes the broth amber.
  2. Cover bones with cold water. Bring slowly to simmer. Skim carefully for 20 minutes.
  3. Add all aromatics. Simmer uncovered, very gently, 3.5 hours.
  4. Strain through fine cloth. The broth must be completely clear. Season with salt, white pepper, saffron.
  5. Soft-boil eggs 6 minutes. Transfer to cold water immediately. Peel gently — keep whole.
  6. Reheat broth to near simmer. Ladle into bowls. Float two eggs. Thread herb oil. Dill on top.
