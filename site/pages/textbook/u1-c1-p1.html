Phonology & the Sound System

1.1 The Five Vowels

1.1

The Five Vowels

Vaelic has five vowels. Each one has exactly one sound, the same in every word, every syllable, every position. There are no long/short distinctions, no vowel shifts, no silent vowels. A vowel is always pronounced exactly as described below — without exception, for life.

A
/ah/
as in "father"
E
/eh/
as in "bed"
I
/ee/
as in "machine"
O
/oh/
as in "go"
U
/oo/
as in "rule"
Why This Matters

In many languages, knowing how a vowel is spelled does not tell you how to pronounce it. In English, the letter "a" in "father", "fate", "fall", and "about" represents four different sounds. In Vaelic, this never happens. The letter A is always /ah/. The letter E is always /eh/. What you see is what you say — always, in every word, for as long as the language exists.
