3.2 Verbs Never Conjugate

3.2

Verbs Never Conjugate

In Vaelic, a verb is a verb. It does not change form based on who the subject is. Doro means "go" when the subject is I, you, she, we, or they. The subject pronoun or noun tells you who is acting; the verb tells you what the action is. These two roles are kept completely separate.

No Conjugation — Ever

The same verb form for every subject:

Doro mina. — I go.    Doro ven. — You go.    Doro narev. — He/she goes.

Doro sena. — We go.    Doro venet. — You all go.    Doro naret. — They go.

This applies to every verb in the language without a single exception. You never need to memorise conjugation tables in Vaelic.
