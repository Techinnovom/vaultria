Pronouns & Identity

2.1 The Six Pronouns

2.1

The Six Pronouns

Vaelic uses six pronouns to cover all combinations of person, number, and social orientation. Notice that every pronoun begins with a Soft-class sound — N, M, S, V — because people are the subject of Soft-class words. This is not coincidence; it is the Sound Contract at work.

VaelicPronunciationMeaningExampleNotes
MinaMI-naI / me / selfKavi mina. — I speak.M opening — the primary self; soft, warm, present
VenVENyou (singular)Ke doro ven? — Are you going?V opening — directed toward another individual
NarevNA-revhe / she / they (singular)Kavi narev. — They speak.No gender. Always the same word for any individual
SenaSE-nawe / usDoro sena ka nod. — We go to the city.S opening — the collective in motion, flowing
VenetVE-netyou (plural)Ke kavi venet? — Do you all speak?Ven (you sg.) + -et (plural suffix)
NaretNA-retthey / them (plural)Doro naret ka meto. — They go home.Narev (they sg.) + -et (plural suffix). Note: the -ev drops before -et
The -et Plural Suffix

The suffix -et marks any pronoun as plural. It attaches to the base pronoun: Ven (you sg.) → Venet (you pl.). For Narev, the -ev is replaced: Narev → Naret. This is the only pronoun transformation in Vaelic — and the only one you ever need.
