7.3 Family Structures in Vaultrian Culture

7.3

Family Structures in Vaultrian Culture

The Vaelic family vocabulary reflects Vaultrian social norms. Sorika (family) is broadly defined — it encompasses any stable living group bonded by care and mutual obligation, whether biological, adoptive, or chosen. The language has no word for "blood relative" as distinct from "chosen family" because Vaultrian law makes no such distinction.

Key Family Terms in Context

Sorika mina kala. — My family is good. (general statement)

Sorinel mina teranel. — My parent is a teacher.

Kavi mina se sorilori davaseno. — I spoke with my sibling yesterday.

Moritek lorimora lori sorika. — The family guardian protects the child.

Selora moravel-nara meto kenu. — My loved one arrives home soon.

Dialogue 7.1 — Meeting the Community
Setting: A new citizen attends their first community gathering (Melat meeting) in their new district.
Kavinel
Kala nera, vena melat. Riva mina nara velan — varo velan lo nodelat.
Hello, community people. I see a new person — a new citizen in the neighbourhood.
Varo
Mina Varo Lena. Da-selora mina de nod Lunor senolek dava. Mina kera — no-ta-zari mina naret venet.
I am Citizen Lena. I arrived from Lunor city a week ago. I am a stranger — I have not yet come to know you all.
Nazirel
No-kera ven nera — varo ven lo nodelat. Selora naret kenu: sorilori mina na nodel ven.
You are no longer a stranger — you are a citizen in the neighbourhood. They arrive soon: my sibling and your neighbour.
Varo
Kalanek, nazirel. Mina moravel-nara na lori donek — sorika mina trenek.
Thank you, elder. I have a loved one and two children — my family numbers three.
Kavinel
Kala. Sorika kala melat. Moritek sena navol.
Good. Family makes good community. We always protect each other.
Culture Note · The Transition from Kera to Rena

In Vaultrian culture, the moment when a stranger becomes a friend is considered significant enough to be named. The word Dorokat (return) is used metaphorically for this transition: Dorokat Kera ka Rena — "The stranger returns to being a friend." The use of Dorokat signals that friendship is not a destination but a recognition of something that was always possible — the stranger was always a potential friend; the transition is a journey back to that potential.

Formal community introductions (as in the dialogue above) are an established Vaultrian ritual. New residents of any district are expected to attend at least one community gathering within their first month and introduce themselves using their name, origin city, family composition, and professional role. This is not intrusive — it is civic orientation, and the community is expected to reciprocate with practical assistance (orientation to local resources, introductions to relevant neighbours and services).
