5.4 Quantifiers — The Complete Set

5.4

Quantifiers — The Complete Set

Vaelic quantifiers express approximate or general quantity: many, few, all, none, some, enough. All begin with Nek-, the quantity root. Like numbers, they follow the noun they quantify.

VaelicPronunciationMeaningExample
Nektoranek-TO-ramany / a lotNektora vena lo nod. — Many people in the city.
Neklinanek-LI-nafew / not manyNeklina zanek lo satok. — Few resources in the system.
Nekmoratnek-MO-ratmuch / a great amountNekmorat tera selora sena. — Much time before we arrive.
Nekdavnek-DAVall / everyNekdav varo doro ka zelek. — All citizens go to the place.
Neknonek-NOnone / nothingNekno zanek lo meto. — No resources at home.
Nekmiranek-MI-rasome / a fewNekmira rena selora nera. — Some friends arrive now.
Nekbinaknek-BI-nakenough / sufficientNekbinak serokav lo meto. — Enough food at home.
Nekvelnek-VELmoreMora ven nekvel zanek. — Give more resources.
Nekmoratvelnek-mo-rat-VELmajorityNekmoratvel varo kavi kala. — The majority of citizens speak well.
Nekvekonek-VE-kominority / few of manyNekveko varo no-doro. — A minority of citizens do not go.
Nekteranek-TE-rahow many / how muchKena nektera varo? — How many citizens?
Nekbireknek-BI-reka tally / a countBirek mina nekbirek varo. — I build a count of citizens.
