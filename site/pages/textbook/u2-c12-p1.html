12.1 Introduction & Sound Class Notes

12.1

Introduction & Sound Class Notes

Vaelic descriptors encode the Sound Contract: positive/open qualities use Soft-class openings (Kala, Melan, Serinala), strong/technical qualities use Hard-class (Kirat, Morat, Velan), danger/restriction uses Sharp-class (Zelonek, Torvak, Toravel), and movement/time qualities use Flow-class (Navol, Siret, Daran).

The Comparison System — Vel and Nek

Vel = more / comparative: placed directly before the descriptor

Nek = most / superlative: placed directly before the descriptor

Nod kala. — A good city.    Nod vel kala. — A better city.    Nod nek kala. — The best city.

Ralet torek. — A large building.    Ralet vel torek. — A larger building.

Kavi mina vel kirat. — I speak more clearly.    Kavi ven nek kirat. — You speak most clearly.

There is no grammatical change to the descriptor — only Vel or Nek is added before it. The word form is always the same.
