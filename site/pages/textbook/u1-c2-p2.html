2.2 No Gendered Pronouns — By Design

2.2

No Gendered Pronouns — By Design

Vaelic has one singular third-person pronoun: Narev. It refers to any individual, regardless of their gender or identity. This is not a recent update or political accommodation — it was a deliberate founding choice. The Language Authority's founding document states: "A language that requires speakers to know the gender of a person before they can name them places an unnecessary burden on speech. We refuse it."

In practice: where English says "he went to the city" or "she arrived home," Vaelic says Doro narev ka nod and Selora narev meto. If gender information is relevant and known, it is provided through context, not grammar.
