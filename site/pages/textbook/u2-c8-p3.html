8.3 Spatial Expressions — Going Beyond Lo/Ka/De

8.3

Spatial Expressions — Going Beyond Lo/Ka/De

While Lo, Ka, and De carry most spatial meaning, Vaelic also uses compound expressions for more precise spatial description.

Compound Spatial Expressions

Ka meto de nod — toward home from the city (a journey home)

Lo zelek de taro — at the place reached via the path

De zelek ka zelek — from place to place (travelling between locations)

Lo binak nod — within the city network (interconnected city zones)

Ka vekat de seket — to the zone from the district (zone-specific movement)

Dialogue 8.1 — Navigating Aurex
Setting: A stranger (Kera) asks a citizen (Varo) for directions to the civic center. This dialogue demonstrates the full preposition system in navigation context.
Kera
Kala nera. Kero zelek varozelek? No-zari mina nod Aurex.
Hello. Where is the civic center? I don't know Aurex city.
Varo
Doro ven ka taronel nekvek — taronel torek. Riva ven ralet velan torek lo noddekat. Varozelek lo ralet velan trenekvek.
Go to the first highway — the large highway. You will see a large new building at the city center. The civic center is in the third large new building.
Kera
Kenu selora mina lo varozelek de keno nera?
How soon will I arrive at the civic center from where I am now?
Varo
Nolev — mira lovnek. No-doro ra — kelo lo keletzel seketzel donekvek.
Slowly — seven moments. Don't rush — stop at the second border zone checkpoint.
Kera
Kalanek. No-kera mina ven leru — rena mina ven.
Thank you. I will no longer be a stranger to you later — you are my friend.
