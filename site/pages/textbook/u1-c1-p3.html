Phonology & the Sound System

1.3 The Four Phonological Classes

1.3

The Four Phonological Classes

This is the most important feature of Vaelic phonology. Vaelic words are not assigned sounds arbitrarily — they are built from sounds that match their meaning. The four classes create what Vaultrian linguists call the Sound Contract: when a speaker hears a word begin with certain sounds, they already know something about what the word will mean.

Sharp Class
K · T · D · Z · R
Commands, danger, urgency, precision. Words in this class are often short, clipped, and front-loaded with energy.
KAT · DRAK · TORN · ZEL · TOVA
KAT (stop) · DRAK (warn/danger)
ZERIKA (enemy) · KATAVEL (guard)
Soft Class
N · M · L
People, relationships, warmth, the family of the living. Open vowels A and E dominate. Words feel approachable.
NARA · MINA · LORI · RENA
MORAVEL (love) · SORIKA (family)
NAZIREL (elder) · MELAT (community)
Hard Class
V · P · B
Technology, systems, structures, precision engineering. Words often end in hard stops: -ek, -at, -nek, -ok.
VATEK · BIREK · SATOK · PAVI
BINAK (network) · KAVEL (tool)
BIREKEL (builder) · LETATSATOK (database)
Flow Class
S · G + flowing vowels
Time, movement, progression, change. Words feel like they are moving. Often end in soft landings: -ora, -elu, -al.
SELORA · SENO · NERA · DORO
DOLUN (move toward) · VARUN (future)
SENOMIRA (dawn) · TERAVEL (era)
The Sound Contract — Learn This Well

When a Vaelic speaker hears a word beginning with K or T in a short clipped form, they expect urgency or a command. When they hear a word beginning with N or M with open vowels, they expect a person or relationship. When V, P, or B opens a word with a hard ending, they expect a system or device. When S opens with flowing vowels, they expect time or movement.

This is not decoration. This is architecture. Every new word you encounter, you already know something about — before you look it up.
