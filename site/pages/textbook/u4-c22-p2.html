22.2 Extended Patterns

22.2

Extended Patterns

Pattern 22.1 — Sequential Narrative
SENTENCE 1 · SENTENCE 2 · SENTENCE 3 (juxtaposition = sequence)
Da-selora mina de nod Lunor. Da-riva mina rena donek. Da-kavi sena. Ta-dorokat mina meto. → I arrived from Lunor. I saw two friends. We spoke. I have returned home.
Pattern 22.2 — Compound Subject
VERB + SUBJECT A + Na + SUBJECT B + OBJECT ...
Doro mina na rena ka nod. → I and a friend go to the city.
Birek tova na tovamela satok velan. → The leader and council build a new system.
Pattern 22.3 — Compound Predicate
VERB1 + SUBJECT + ... + Na + VERB2 + [SUBJECT implied] + ...
Doro mina ka nod na riva mina rena. → I go to the city and I see a friend.
Selora rena na kavi rena se mina. → A friend arrives and the friend speaks with me.
Dialogue 22.1 — A Complex Report
Setting: A Vovanel (official) gives a complex multi-clause situation report to the Tovamela (council).
Vovanel
Kavizari mina ka venet: da-selora vena torek de nod Nerath na da-kelo naret lo seket trenek lovnek seno dava.
I formally report to you all: a large number of people arrived from Nerath city and they stayed in the seventh third district seven days ago.
Tova
Ke ta-zerit mina sena zeritnelek lo varotaro? Kenozari no-ta-kavizari ven leru?
Have we analyzed the regulation in the civic process? Why did you not report sooner?
Vovanel
No-ta-selora letat kala ka mina dava — nirat satok. Ta-zeritlen mina navol — ta-zari mina nera. Va-binakel mina zeritbinak na va-kavizari mina ka venet varunseno.
Good data did not arrive to me before — the system was unclear. I have always verified — I now know. I will connect the knowledge network and I will formally report to you all tomorrow.
