4.2 The Seven Question Words

4.2

The Seven Question Words

Each question word replaces the unknown element in the sentence at the appropriate position. The question word goes where the answer would go. The rest of the sentence structure remains identical to the statement form.

Question WordPronunciationMeaningPositionExample
KeKEyes/no markerSentence beginningKe doro ven? — Are you going?
KenaKE-nawhatReplaces the objectKena riva ven? — What do you see?
KeroKE-rowhereReplaces the locationKero doro ven? — Where are you going?
KetiKE-tiwhenReplaces the timeKeti selora rena? — When does the friend arrive?
KenoKE-nowhoReplaces the subjectKeno kavi? — Who speaks?
Nekatine-KA-tihowModifies the verb or objectNekati birek ven? — How do you build?
Kenozarike-no-ZA-riwhy (lit: "who knows")Sentence beginningKenozari doro narev? — Why does he go?
Kenozari — The Vaelic "Why"

The word for "why" is Kenozari — literally "who-knows." This compound (Keno = who + Zari = know) reflects Vaultrian epistemic philosophy: questions of reason are questions of knowledge, and seeking the "why" is the act of asking who holds the understanding. It is one of the most philosophically loaded words in everyday Vaelic speech.

Kenozari doro narev ka zelek torvak? — Why does he go to a restricted area?

Kenozari zerat satok? — Why is the system broken?
