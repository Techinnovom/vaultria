3.5 Understanding Compound Verbs

3.5

Understanding Compound Verbs

Many Vaelic verbs are compounds — they combine two roots to create a new, specific meaning. Understanding the parts helps you decode unfamiliar verbs and guess accurately at meaning. The pattern is always: first root establishes domain, second root specifies the action within that domain.

Compound Verb Anatomy

Birekkat = Birek (build) + Kat (stop/halt) → "build-stop" = demolish (to stop the building from existing)

Dorokat = Doro (go) + Kat (stop) → "go-stop" = return (to go until stopped and come back)

Kaviterina = Kavi (speak) + Terina (teach) → "speak-teach" = explain (to speak in order to teach)

Paviriket = Pavi (use/operate) + Riket (signal) → "operate-signal" = broadcast

Lavidrak = Lavi (hear/listen) + Drak (warn/danger) → "hear-danger" = intercept a signal

Dialogue 3.1 — At the FlowNet Station
Setting: Two citizens meet at a public transit station. This dialogue demonstrates verb-first word order in natural conversation.
Varo A
Kero doro ven nera?
Where are you going right now?
Varo B
Doro mina ka nod Zerex. Riva mina rena mina lo dekat nod.
I go to Zerex city. I will see my friend at the city hub.
Varo A
Zari mina narev — birekel kala. Birek narev satok velan lo Zerex.
I know them — a good engineer. They build a new system in Zerex.
Varo B
Kavi mina se narev seno dava. Teri narev mina zerit binak torek.
I spoke with them yesterday. They teach me about large network data.
Varo A
Kala. Savi mina. Doro kala.
Good. I understand. Go well.
