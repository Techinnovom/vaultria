21.1 The Compound Word Rule

21.1

The Compound Word Rule

A Vaelic compound word is formed by joining two root words. Root A + Root B = word meaning "A acting on, defined by, or in the context of B." The meaning is always traceable — if you know the roots, you can decode any compound.

Compound Formation — The Rules

1. No hyphen needed for two-root compounds: Nod + Dekat = Noddekat (city center)

2. Three-root compounds use the same rule twice: Serokav + Mela = Serokavmela, then if needed: root + Serokavmela

4. The compound preserves the stress of the FINAL element: Noddekat → stress on DE-kat portion (NO-dde-kat)

5. Meaning is always additive from left to right: first root sets domain, second specifies
