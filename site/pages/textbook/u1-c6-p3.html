The Complete Time Vocabulary — 28 Words

Time words in Vaelic all belong to the Flow class (S and L openings, flowing vowels, -era/-ora/-ol endings). They are placed at the end of a sentence in the time slot — after location — as established by the sentence formula.

Dialogue 6.1 — Planning Across Time
Setting: Two Vaultrian planners discuss a multi-phase project. This dialogue demonstrates all four tense markers in natural use.
Birekel A
Keti da-birek sena satok nekvek?
When did we build the first system?
Birekel B
Da-birek sena satok nekvek lo teravel daran. Ta-pavi sena — satok kala morat nera.
We built the first system in the old era. We have operated it — the system is now strong and good.
↳ Da- = past event · Ta- = completed action still felt now
Birekel A
Ke va-birek sena satok donekvek lo senolek varun?
Will we build the second system next week?
Birekel B
No-va-birek sena varunseno — leru. Nemi mina nera: birek sena satok donekvek lo miraseno trenekvek.
We will not build tomorrow — later. I think now: we build the second system at the noon of the third day.
Birekel A
Kala. Pavi mina terasatok nera. Deket mina mira velan.
Good. I use the schedule now. I record the new moment.
Dialogue 6.2 — Telling a Story from Memory
Setting: An elder (Nazirel) tells a younger citizen (Lori) about something that happened in the past. The continuous and completed tenses appear frequently.
Lori
Keti da-selora kiratvel ka nod Aurex?
When did truth arrive in Aurex city?
Nazirel
Doroen sena lo lunok daran — nektora mira. Ta-selora sena lo senomira. No-ta-doro sena noravu.
We were walking through the old night — many moments. We have arrived at dawn. We have never not-arrived.
Lori
Ke ta-zari ven kiratvel dava?
Did you come to know the truth before?
Nazirel
Terien mina navol — ta-zari mina nera. Kiratvel navol. Doroen mina ka ven nera.
I have always been learning — I now know. Truth is permanent. I am arriving toward you now.
Culture Note · Time and the Vaultrian Worldview

The tense system reflects something fundamental about Vaultrian philosophy. The language separates "past events" (Da-) from "completed states" (Ta-) because Vaultrians believe these are different realities. A past event is history — it happened. A completed state is present — its effects are with us now. Da-doro is a fact in the archive. Ta-doro is a condition that shapes right now.

The word Navol — always/permanent — is the most philosophically loaded time word. It appears in the national motto, in legal oaths, in declarations of love, and in architectural inscriptions. Permanence is not aspiration in Vaultria — it is acknowledgment. Kiratvel navol does not say "truth should be permanent." It says "truth is permanent." This is not a wish. It is a statement of ontology.
