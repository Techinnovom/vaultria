6.1 The Four Tense Markers

6.1

The Four Tense Markers

Tense in Vaelic is expressed by prefixing (or suffixing) a marker to the verb. The base verb remains unchanged. The marker changes. This means once you know a verb, you can express it in any tense immediately, with zero memorisation of new forms.

MarkerTense NameApplied to Doro (go)EnglishWhen to Use
(none)Present / habitualDoro mina.I go. / I am going. / I go (as a habit).Current actions, habits, general truths
Da-PastDa-doro mina.I went. / I was going.Actions completed at a point in the past
Va-FutureVa-doro mina.I will go. / I am going to go.Actions that will happen after the present
Ta-Completed (perfect)Ta-doro mina.I have gone. / I have completed going.Actions finished whose result is felt now
-enContinuous (suffix)Doroen mina.I am [in the process of] going.Actions ongoing at the moment of speech
Key Distinction — Da- (Past) vs Ta- (Completed)

This distinction is one of the most important in advanced Vaelic and must be learned precisely.

Da- describes a past action: when it happened, not its current state. Da-doro mina davaseno. — I went yesterday. (I left at a point in the past)

Ta- describes a completed action whose result is present now: Ta-doro mina. — I have gone. (The going is finished; I am now somewhere different as a result) Ta-birek sena satok. — We have built the system. (The building is complete; the system now exists)

In Vaultrian law and official records, this distinction is legally significant. A da-kavi statement (they spoke, past) differs from a ta-kavi statement (they have spoken, the record is complete) in terms of accountability and timestamp.
