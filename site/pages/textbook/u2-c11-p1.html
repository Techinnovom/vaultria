11.1 Introduction & Sound Class Notes

11.1

Introduction & Sound Class Notes

Emotion words in Vaelic follow a systematic pattern: they combine a root feeling (kalemi = happiness, dralun = fear, moravel = love) with suffixes that specify intensity and quality. The -vek suffix marks a more active/dynamic version of an emotion; -mi marks a contemplative, inward version; -nek marks gratitude-adjacent or relational forms.

The Inner State Pattern — Lo + Emotion Word

All Vaelic emotions follow the same pattern: Lo [emotion] mina — "In [emotion], I." This is identical to the health state pattern from Chapter 9. The consistency is deliberate — the language treats emotional and physical states as parallel realities, both expressed with Lo.

Lo kalemi mina. — I am in joy. / I feel joyful.

Lo moravel mina. — I am in love. / I feel love.

Lo dralun mina. — I am in fear. / I feel fear.

Lo serinal mina. — I feel calm. / I am at peace.

To describe someone else's emotion, replace mina with the appropriate pronoun: Lo tekalon narev. — He/she is in anger.
