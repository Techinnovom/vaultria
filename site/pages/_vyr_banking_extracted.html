Vaultria's financial system is state-controlled, anti-speculative, and built around a single principle: citizens should never be trapped by money they never had. Physical VYR exists alongside digital wallets. There is no credit. There is no debt except one — and that one carries no interest.

Section 01 — Currency

VYR — The Currency of Vaultria

Vel kadir morven — "The permanent measure"

VYR is the sole legal currency of the Sovereign State of Vaultria. It is issued exclusively by the Karelth Financial Authority, housed in the Karelth district of Velora zone. VYR exists in two identical forms — physical and digital — with no distinction in value or legal status between them. One physical VYR equals one digital VYR. Always.

VYR is not speculative. It cannot be traded on any exchange. It does not fluctuate in value against itself. There are no VYR derivatives, no VYR futures, and no investment products denominated in VYR. The Karelth Financial Authority sets the value of VYR against foreign currencies monthly, based on a basket of reference currencies — not on market forces.

Absolute Prohibition
No cryptocurrency, no digital token, no speculative financial instrument may be denominated in VYR or traded for VYR within Vaultrian territory. Operating such an instrument is a Class I financial crime under Codex Vol. IV, Art. IV.1.2.

Physical VYR notes carry the national seal on the obverse and a zone name on the reverse — each denomination features a different zone. The 500 VYR note features Velora. The 1 VYR note features Varex — a deliberate statement that the smallest unit of the currency represents the zone that produces the food that sustains all others.

Physical VYR — anonymous use
Physical VYR requires no CID for transactions. Cash is anonymous by design. Vaultria believes citizens have the right to spend money privately. The state does not surveil physical cash transactions.
Digital VYR — identity-linked
All digital transactions are linked to the sender's and receiver's CID. Digital VYR cannot be transferred anonymously. This is the explicit trade-off for digital access — convenience for traceability.

Section 02 — Citizen Wallet

The Citizen Wallet — Vel Kadir

Vel kadir sena — "The measured holding"

Every Vaultrian citizen holds exactly one VYR wallet, activated at their Veloran on their 18th birthday. The wallet is inseparable from the CID — it cannot be transferred, duplicated, or held by a third party. Children under 18 are covered by a custodial sub-wallet held under their parent or guardian's account, which transfers to the child automatically at the Veloran.

Section 03 — Savings

Savings Tiers

Vel morven kadir — "Holding for the future"

Vaultria encourages saving but does not mandate it. The two savings tiers offer state-set interest rates — competitive enough to reward long-term holding, structured enough to prevent the savings system from becoming a vehicle for wealth accumulation beyond functional need. The 200,000 VYR Vault ceiling is the most significant constraint: it reflects Vaultria's position that savings should secure a citizen's future, not generate passive income at scale.

Inheritance & Vault Balance
Upon a citizen's death, their Vault Balance is transferred to named beneficiaries declared in the Vel Morven Directive or their citizen record. If no beneficiary is named, the balance transfers to the surviving household or, absent that, to the Civic Fund. Inheritance does not count as income and is not subject to the income ceiling.

Section 05 — Income Ceilings

Income Ceilings & Enforcement

Vel kadir morven — "The measured maximum"

Income ceilings are set by Merit Tier for all registered civic employment. They prevent runaway wage inequality within Vaultrian civic domains. They do not apply to savings growth, gifts, inheritance, or private transactions. The ceiling limits what a citizen can earn from civic employment — not what they can hold or save.

TierIncome CeilingHigh-Need Domain Bonus
T1 — Vel TarinStandard domain band — set annually by Civic Board per domainNot applicable
T2 — Vel Kadir+25% above T1 ceiling for the same domain+15% above T2 ceiling
T3 — Vel Sorex+50% above T1 ceiling; eligible for highest domain band+20% above T3 ceiling
Registered employment
All wages processed via Karelth Payroll. The system physically cannot process a wage above the citizen's ceiling — the payment is blocked at source. The employer is notified. There is no override.
Self-employment (Nara Birek)
Annual income declaration. The citizen declares total earnings. Any amount above their ceiling is automatically transferred to the Civic Fund — not penalised, simply redirected.
What is NOT capped
Gifts, inheritance, Vault Balance interest returns, private property transactions, and foreign currency holdings are not subject to income ceilings. The ceiling is specifically an employment income mechanism.
Ceiling overages — Civic Fund
VYR above a citizen's ceiling that is redirected to the Civic Fund is used for public infrastructure, healthcare, education, and civic programmes. Overages are publicly reported in aggregate annually.

Section 06 — Business Accounts

Nara Birek Accounts

Nara Birek — "The registered flow"

Every registered Nara Birek enterprise holds a Birek Account — entirely separate from the owner's personal wallet. The Birek Account has three sub-accounts built in by design. The most important structural feature is the Tax Reserve: 18% of all income received into a Birek Account is automatically held in the Tax Reserve and cannot be moved to the Operating Account. Tax is never owed at the end of the year — it is held throughout the year.

Birek Operating
Daily Business Flow
No Interest
All business income, expenses, supplier payments, and day-to-day transactions. Full access. No lock period.
Tax Reserve
Auto-Held — Cannot be moved

18% of every VYR received is held here automatically. Cannot be transferred to Operating. Released at annual tax declaration. Moving this balance is a Class II offense.
Payroll Account
Wage Processing
Via Karelth Payroll
All employee wages are processed from here through the Karelth Payroll System. Ceiling enforcement is applied automatically at this stage.
Multiple account holders
Partnerships and co-operatives may have multiple CIDs with access to a single Birek Account. Every transaction is attributed to a specific CID at point of execution — shared access does not obscure individual action.
Closing a business
At business dissolution, the Tax Reserve is settled, remaining Operating balance transfers to the owner's personal wallet, and the Birek Account is permanently closed. The record remains in the Karelth audit trail indefinitely.

Section 07 — Foreign Exchange

Foreign Currency Exchange

Vel kadir sora — "Measuring against the outside"

VYR is not freely floating. Its exchange rate against foreign currencies is set monthly by the Karelth Financial Authority using a basket of reference currencies. Citizens and businesses may exchange VYR, but within defined limits designed to prevent capital flight and currency speculation.

Section 08 — Debt

The Civic Bridge Loan

Vel kadir narovel — "The temporary measure"

Vaultria has no credit system. Citizens cannot borrow against future income. There are no private lending institutions. Citizens cannot charge interest on money lent to other citizens. The Civic Bridge Loan is the single and only exception — and it exists because Vaultria distinguishes between debt that traps people and debt that supports them through an involuntary crisis.

No Credit — Absolute Position
There are no credit cards, no overdraft facilities, no buy-now-pay-later schemes, and no private lending in Vaultria. Any private citizen who charges interest on money lent to another citizen commits a Class II financial crime under Art. IV.2.4. This is not a restriction — it is a founding principle of the Vaultrian economy.
The Civic Bridge Loan — Vel Narovel Kadir
"The temporary record-holding" — a measure held on behalf of a citizen until they can carry it themselves

Issued by: Karelth Financial Authority exclusively. No other institution may issue a Bridge Loan. The process takes 5 business days from application to disbursement.

Eligibility: The citizen must be in documented involuntary financial hardship — job loss, medical emergency, or a crisis that was not the result of reckless spending. A citizen who overspent is not eligible. A citizen who lost their income because their employer collapsed is. The distinction is deliberate. Vaultria does not rescue poor decisions. It supports genuine misfortune.

Additional eligibility requirements: citizen must have been registered in Vaultria for at least 2 years, and must have no existing Bridge Loan outstanding.

Maximum amount: Three months of the citizen's current income ceiling. Not three months of what they actually earned — three months of what they are entitled to earn at their tier. This gives citizens in crisis access to meaningful support even if their recent income was below ceiling.

No interest. The Bridge Loan is returned in full — exactly what was borrowed, nothing more. Vaultria does not profit from a citizen's crisis. The state issues the loan to fulfil its obligation to citizen welfare, not to generate revenue.

Repayment: Automatic deduction from wage at 10% per month once employment resumes. The repayment cannot exceed 18 months total. If the citizen has not fully repaid within 18 months, the outstanding balance is reviewed by the Karelth Authority — extension may be granted or, in exceptional circumstances, the remainder may be written off.

Cannot be used for business. Bridge Loans are personal survival instruments. They may not fund a Nara Birek enterprise or any commercial activity.

Section 09 — Audit & Integrity

Audit Trail & Anti-Corruption

Vel sorex narovel — "The permanent financial record"

Every digital VYR transaction is permanently logged. The record cannot be deleted, altered, or hidden. Even reversed transactions — refunds, corrections — appear as two entries: the original and the reversal. There are no deletions in the Vaultrian financial record. This is a foundational design decision, not a policy choice.

Separation Principle
The Civic Board has no access to financial records. This is a deliberate firewall — the body that manages civic contribution, Merit Tier, and zone governance cannot see a citizen's financial activity. Financial oversight is exclusively Karelth Authority and, when legally required, the High Tribunal.
Auto-Flag Triggers
Single transaction above 10,000 VYR — auto-flagged, declaration required at time of transaction. Karelth reviews within 7 days.
30-day cumulative total above 50,000 VYR — mandatory audit review triggered automatically. Citizen is notified.
Structuring — repeated transfers just below declaration thresholds (e.g. multiple 990 VYR transfers in sequence). System detects pattern, flags for review.
Birek account receiving income without Tax Reserve allocation — technically impossible by design, but any system bypass triggers immediate freeze and review.
Transfer from a frozen account — accounts frozen under Class I or II sanctions cannot transact. Any attempted transfer is blocked and logged.
Gifts above 5,000 VYR between the same two parties in one year — soft flag, reviewed to ensure the gift is genuine and not a disguised wage payment.
Repeated large physical VYR deposits — deposits above 5,000 VYR require CID confirmation. Multiple large cash deposits in a short period trigger a soft audit review.
Immutability Principle
No VYR transaction can be deleted from the audit record. Refunds and corrections appear as separate entries alongside the original — the original is never removed. This applies to all parties including the Karelth Authority itself. The financial record of Vaultria is permanent.
